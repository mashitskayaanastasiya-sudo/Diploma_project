Compact Global GEM Practice EPNG

This compact does not supersede other Firm policies or guidelines. Partners and CSTs remain responsible for complying with the Client Service Policy, M&A and Serving Competitors guidance, and any other relevant sector compacts for the specific client or engagement context.

Work that is not approved includes:

- Nuclear energy work with clients or in nascent markets with clear dual-use ambition.
- Work helping clients solidify, advocate, or justify a position denying climate change.
- Work supporting clients in circumventing environmental or health and safety standards or targets.
- Overhead transmission and distribution work in California, except work that excludes wildfire-risk topics and is subject to CSRC approval.

High-risk work requires CSRC approval with input from PRACL and other relevant cell leaders, as well as ongoing deliverable review. Examples include:

- Nuclear energy work with clients or in markets with potential dual-use concerns.
- Work on nuclear waste disposal.
- Work on new coal power plant builds.
- Overhead transmission and distribution work in locations classified as high or very high wildfire risk by the relevant authority.
- Serving industry associations.
- Operations work with clients that recently experienced a major operational crisis, such as an incident with significant environmental impact or casualties.
- Work on coal with explicit objectives to increase availability or extend the life of a plant. Security of supply, affordability concerns, and social communities at risk are key considerations for approval.

Medium-risk plus work requires CSRC approval with input from PRACL and other relevant cell leaders. Examples include:

- All nuclear work not otherwise prohibited or classified as high risk.
- Large-scale new-build hydro projects with significant societal or environmental impact, such as relocation.
- New-build conventional gas work.

Medium-risk work requires PRACL approval with input from other relevant cell leaders. Examples include:

- Work on health, safety, and environmental issues, including safety program assessments, cost-cutting, or efficiency work.
- Pricing engagements that affect a population's ability to receive basic utilities.
- Commodity marketing, trading, commercial optimization, or trading risk management advice.
- Privatization of state-owned utilities.

Wildfire-risk topics include vegetation management, wildfire mitigation plans, incident response or investigation, inspection and maintenance, asset prioritization, and safety practices, systems, or management.

Thermal coal work has additional restrictions. Work that is not approved includes:

- Thermal coal work in high-risk countries where the Firm has no office.
- Work inconsistent with national or local public policy commitments.
- Growth or extension of assets below acceptable safety or environmental standards.
- Advice to counter NGOs or regulatory implications of sustainability policies.
- Work for a greenfield thermal coal mine in OECD or EU countries.

High-risk thermal coal work requires CSRC approval with input from PRACL and other relevant leaders, as well as ongoing deliverable review. Examples include:

- Thermal coal work in high-risk countries where the Firm has an office.
- Work on projects where reputable investment funds will not invest.
- Work for a greenfield thermal coal mine in non-OECD or non-EU countries.

Any thermal coal mining work not otherwise addressed requires an environmental benefit and/or energy security or affordability benefit. It should be reviewed by the Mining Practice, with a higher bar for clients whose coal output mostly goes to OECD or EU countries.

Medium-risk plus or medium-risk thermal coal work may include:

- Work on coal with explicit objectives to increase availability or extend the life of a generation plant.
- Politically sensitive topics, including policy-related support.
- Engagements with direct impact on local communities.
- Adjustments that increase asset emissions.
- Work that increases use of high-impurity coal.

Market intelligence without accompanying advice on strategy or operations is lower risk.
