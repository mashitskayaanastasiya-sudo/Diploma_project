U.S. Public and Social Sector Handbook

Serving Public Sector clients gives Firm members the opportunity to create distinctive impact, including improving government services, increasing the efficiency of government agencies, and supporting government missions. Public Sector work also involves different requirements and larger impact risks than Private Sector work.

The Firm has policies and procedures to manage risks unique to the Public Sector. This handbook provides additional guidance for Firm members performing Public Sector work in the United States. Leaders should seek counsel from COMPANY USG Partners and the USG Operations and Go-To-Market teams to understand applicable rules and requirements.

Public Sector work refers to Federal, Regional, State, and Local Government departments and agencies, and other public bodies, including government nonprofits.

Public Sector work:

- Is governed by extensive and varied rules, laws, regulations, USG policies, and Firm-wide policies.
- Requires strict compliance with contractual requirements and the statement of work for the engagement.
- Is more likely to be disclosed publicly because of public disclosure laws and regulations.

The Firm generally proposes on, contracts for, and performs U.S. Public Sector work through COMPANY, Inc. Washington D.C., known internally as USG or COMPANY USG. This includes work for Federal, State, and Local government clients, and work supported in whole or in part with government funding.

Engagements that must open in USG include:

- Work for a Federal government agency.
- Work for a State government agency.
- Work for a City or local government agency.
- Work for a public university or public hospital.
- Work where COMPANY serves a private sector company as a subcontractor on a study with a Federal government agency.

Private universities and private nonprofits are not considered Public Sector entities and may open outside USG, subject to applicable inclusion guidelines. Public-private partnerships may open outside USG, subject to inclusion guidelines, because they involve public funding.

Companies doing business with governmental entities may face significant legal, contractual, record-keeping, and compliance requirements, and may be subject to government audits. COMPANY established USG to navigate and fulfill these governmental requirements.

USG may also perform certain U.S.-based Private Sector work. Any work requiring a U.S. government security clearance must open in USG because of USG's facility security clearance.

USG has several distinguishing requirements:

- Only USG Partners, Senior Partners, or specially authorized Partners can sign proposals, contracts, representations and certifications, or other documents that bind USG.
- USG has its own Code of Ethics and Conduct and Standards of Conduct. USG Partners, employees, personnel, and any Firm member staffed on a U.S. Public Sector engagement must certify acceptance of the Code and complete annual security and risk trainings.
- USG Senior Partners, Partners, and APs must contact the Facility Security Officer for approval before doing any work with a foreign client, whether commercial or government.
- USG has specific pricing standards, including for Private Sector clients.
- USG has specially trained personnel to help teams comply with Public Sector requirements.

The protocol for opening engagements in USG provides an objective and consistent method for determining which studies should open within USG.

An engagement must open in USG if any of the following are true:

- The engagement is for a U.S. government entity at the federal, state, or local level.
- The engagement is for a public university, public hospital, public nonprofit, public utility district, or other public entity.
- COMPANY is a subcontractor on an engagement with a U.S. government entity.

An engagement for a commercial study may open in USG after consultation with the USG President and USG Legal if any of the following are true:

- A USG Partner is the originating ED, Responsible Partner, or Accountable Senior Partner.
- The engagement is for a quasi-governmental U.S. entity.
- The U.S. government is an active participant in the engagement.
- U.S. government funds are being used to pay for the engagement.

When client service teams are considering opening a commercial engagement in USG, the Partners must consult with USG Legal and the USG President. The proposed work will be reviewed to determine whether the project should open in USG or elsewhere within COMPANY. The Responsible Partner or Accountable Senior Partner must follow that determination.

All Firm members must comply with the requirements and associated actions in this procedure and any applicable laws. Failure to comply with this procedure or any Firm policy may result in disciplinary action, including separation from the Firm.
