Policy Against Discrimination and Harassment

All forms of harassment and discrimination are strictly prohibited. Firm members must report any actual or potential violation promptly and in good faith. The Firm prohibits retaliation against anyone for good-faith reporting, inquiries, or participation in an investigation or proceeding.

This policy applies to all Firm members. Prohibited behavior is unacceptable on Firm premises and in any work-related setting outside the Firm, including business meetings, business trips, Firm-sponsored events, recruiting and learning events, and work-related social events.

The policy covers more than the law, so behavior may violate COMPANY policy even where no law has been violated.

The Firm aspires to maintain a work environment that supports, inspires, and respects all colleagues, applicants, and clients. Processes such as recruiting, compensation, training, advancement, and assignment should be merit-based and applied without discrimination.

Firm members must treat colleagues, recruiting applicants, clients, and others with respect, fairness, and dignity. This policy may also apply to conduct outside working hours and locations, including online and social media activity, and conduct by third parties such as clients or vendors if it affects the work environment or relationships.

The Firm prohibits discrimination, harassment, bullying, or other offensive conduct toward any individual based on:

- Race.
- Color.
- Religion.
- National or ethnic origin.
- Citizenship.
- Age.
- Sex.
- LGBTQI+ status.
- Sexual orientation.
- Gender identity or gender expression.
- Pregnancy.
- Disability.
- Military service status.
- Marital status.
- Caste.
- Political affiliation.
- Any other protected characteristic.

The Firm prohibits behavior that makes the work environment hostile, intimidating, or offensive because of or related to a protected characteristic. The Firm also prohibits language that perpetuates stereotypes or condones or incites hatred or violence toward any individual or group based on a protected characteristic.

Discriminatory or harassing behavior may include:

- Unwelcome or unwanted touching or physical contact.
- Offensive gestures.
- Ostracizing or freezing out.
- Threatening or isolating behavior.
- Inappropriate or insensitive comments.
- Malicious gossip, comments, abuse, jokes, ridicule, derogatory nicknames, offensive descriptors, or taunting.
- Intrusive or inappropriate questions or comments about someone's private life, political views, medical condition, or disability.
- Assault or vandalism.

Discriminatory or harassing behavior also includes less favorable treatment for rejecting or submitting to harassment, and offensive materials displayed or shared in the workplace or on Firm devices.

Sexual harassment is prohibited. It means unwelcome or unwanted sexual or romantic advances or sexual or romantic conduct that creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment or unreasonably interferes with work responsibilities. Sexual harassment may involve individuals of the same or different gender.

Sexual harassment may include:

- Unwelcome advances or propositions, including persistent flirtation or social media contact.
- Pressure for sexual or romantic favors.
- Promises made in return for sexual or romantic favors.
- Suggestive, insulting, or sexual comments, jokes, gestures, or innuendos.
- Verbal commentary about an individual's body.
- Discussions of sexual prowess or abilities.
- Leering, whistling, or unwelcome touching.
- Assault or coercion of sexual acts.
- Display of intimate or sexually suggestive objects, devices, or pictures.
- Intrusive questions about a person's private or sex life.

The absence of objection does not constitute consent or mean that behavior is welcome, especially where there is a difference in status between colleagues. Conduct does not need to be explicitly sexual to violate this policy if it is directed at a colleague because of sex, sexual orientation, or gender identity.

The Firm also prohibits exclusionary and harmful mistreatment of colleagues, including:

- Threatening, humiliating, or intimidating behavior.
- Work interference or sabotage.
- Verbal abuse.

Examples of prohibited mistreatment include:

- Slandering, ridiculing, or maligning a person.
- Persistent hurtful, insulting, or humiliating name-calling.
- Using a person as the subject or target of jokes.
- Abusive or offensive remarks.
- Nonverbal gestures that convey threats.
- Socially or physically excluding a person in work-related activities.
- Persistent singling out with intent to exclude.
- Personal insults or offensive nicknames.
- Public humiliation or reprimands.
- Constant criticism unrelated or minimally related to job performance.
- Spreading rumors or gossip.
- Encouraging others to disregard instructions.
- Manipulating a colleague's ability to work.
- Deliberately excluding colleagues from meetings or work-related activities.
- Pressuring or forcing colleagues to consume alcohol or other substances.

Colleagues who experience or witness exclusionary conduct should report it before it escalates or becomes severe or pervasive.

The Firm encourages anyone who experiences or observes conduct that may be inconsistent with laws, Firm policies, or values to voice a concern.

Retaliation is prohibited. Retaliation includes intimidation, reprisal, harassment, detriments, or adverse action based on a complaint or participation in a complaint investigation.

Concerns about potential or actual discrimination, harassment, or retaliation must be reported promptly so the Firm can respond appropriately.

Firm members who witness or suspect a violation must promptly report it to:

- The Regional People Leader, Regional HR Leader, PD, or HR in the region.
- Got a Concern?, where confidential and anonymous reporting is available where permitted by law.
- For partner-related concerns, the Professional Standards Committee or Global Partner Office.

If a Partner, supervisor, Engagement Manager, Associate Partner, equivalent role, or manager observes or receives information about prohibited behavior, they must report it immediately to the relevant HR or PD leader, Global People Risk CoE, Global Partnership Office, or Chief People Officer.

Ombuds are confidential discussion partners for concerns and resolution options. Contacting an Ombud is confidential and does not constitute notice to the Firm for investigation or action.

Firm members must comply with this policy, act with integrity, and report violations. Local law controls where it imposes stricter requirements.

The Firm does not tolerate retaliation for good-faith reporting or participation in a review or investigation.

Failure to comply may result in disciplinary action, including separation from the Firm. Third-party violations may result in consequences up to and including termination of arrangements with the Firm.
