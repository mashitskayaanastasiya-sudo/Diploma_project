Public, Social, and State-owned Sector Standard

The responsible partner is responsible for the engagement. The assigned senior partner is accountable for confirming that applicable risk requirements and guardrails are followed throughout the engagement lifecycle, beginning before a letter of proposal is issued.

This standard applies to work involving public sector, social sector, and state-owned enterprise clients or stakeholders. It applies to paid and unpaid work, formal and informal work, internal and client-facing work, and work performed directly or through third parties.

Enhanced risk requirements may apply to:

- Public sector engagements.
- Social sector engagements with public beneficiaries or material public stakeholders.
- State-owned enterprise engagements identified through Engage or client service risk review.
- Public procurement activity.
- Work that may create freedom of information, public disclosure, political neutrality, conflict, or foreign influence concerns.

Relevant engagement risk information must be registered in Engage as early as possible and before a letter of proposal is issued. Required client service risk review should also occur as early as possible.

For state-owned enterprises, the Firm distinguishes between Type A and Type B state-owned enterprises. The classification affects applicable risk requirements and should be assessed using the relevant Firm guidance.

If a matter involves public procurement, Firm Legal should be consulted when the applicable rules are unclear. The Firm must comply with procurement laws, tender rules, client instructions, and any restrictions on communications or participation.

Before or during a request-for-proposal process, Firm members must not seek or receive an unfair competitive advantage. Firm members should not:

- Use misleading information.
- Seek confidential competitor information.
- Access other bids unless expressly permitted.
- Improperly influence procurement decisions.
- Take actions that could reduce competition.

For sole-source opportunities, the team should assess applicable laws and client rules. The Firm should avoid drafting sole-source justifications unless permitted and should consider litigation, compliance, and reputational risk.

For pro bono or no-fee work in scope of this standard, Firm Legal and the office managing partner should be consulted. Additional escalation may be required depending on the risk profile.

Outcome-based fees that are uncapped are not permitted. Requests for per-diem or hourly rate disclosures should be handled through the appropriate Firm channel. Engagement teams should maintain auditable documentation.

Required contracts or approved alternatives must be in place before work begins. Tender documents, draft contracts, amendments, and litigation-related issues should be reviewed or escalated to Firm Legal when required. Signed contracts should be returned to Legal within required timelines.

Firm members working on in-scope matters must complete required PSSS training within 30 days and renew it every 24 months.

Engagement teams must assess conflicts, confidentiality, personal conflicts, organizational conflicts of interest, public sector conflicts, and any issue that could impair the Firm's objectivity or impartiality.

Teams should consider whether the Firm:

- Helped establish rules for a procurement or decision process.
- Has access to non-public source-selection information.
- Could be viewed as unable to provide objective advice.

Foreign influence laws may apply to certain work. Firm Legal should be consulted when foreign influence obligations may be relevant.

During and after an engagement, work product should maintain objectivity and political neutrality. Teams should avoid recommendations that are political or sensitive policy positions unless the work has been appropriately reviewed and approved.

Work product should use objective, fact-based language and may include options, legends, or disclaimers when appropriate. The responsible partner should complete final review. Firm Communications should be involved for controversial, sensitive, or political work.

If a freedom-of-information request or other public disclosure risk arises, Firm Communications should be notified as required.

Engagement teams should remain alert to anti-corruption risks and speak up if concerns arise.

The Firm does not perform work for political parties, individual political leaders, or legislatures unless a boundary case is reviewed and approved through the required escalation process.

Definitions and additional guidance may apply to:

- Public sector entities.
- Multilateral organizations.
- Social sector organizations.
- Nonprofit organizations.
- State-owned enterprises.
- State-owned enterprise Type A and Type B classifications.
