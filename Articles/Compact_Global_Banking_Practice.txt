Compact Global Banking Practice

This compact identifies banking-related engagement types that are not approved, require CSRC approval, or require PRACL approval with input from relevant cell leaders.

Work that is not approved includes:

- Certifying, assuring, or validating regulatory compliance.
- Drafting or opining on regulations or policies for regulators.
- Meeting with government officials to lobby on behalf of financial institutions or trade associations.
- Endorsing carbon market assets or associating the Firm name with tokenization of carbon credits.
- Performing independent audits or providing formal opinion letters, such as fairness opinions for M&A.

High-risk work requires CSRC approval with input from PRACL and other relevant cell leaders, as well as ongoing deliverable review. Examples include:

- High-risk money movement work for a financial institution in a country on the Financial Action Task Force list of jurisdictions under increased monitoring, especially cross-border financial interaction, international private banking, or financial crimes work such as KYC.
- Work in nascent areas where regulatory and legal uncertainty is high and compliance requirements are immature.
- Work related to financing businesses considered higher risk under the CITIO framework, or industries with heightened legal risks, such as cannabis companies.
- Regulatory remediation work that requires accounting, tax, or legal interpretations, or crisis response work with a high public profile or links to customer harm.
- Financial crimes work that includes operational activities such as individual file review, lookbacks, or SAR filings.
- Work that significantly affects vulnerable populations, such as deeply subprime or financially distressed populations, and may harm those populations or society.
- Turnaround programs for highly distressed financial institutions, including banks with extreme liquidity difficulties.
- Digital assets work, which must be assessed under the Digital Assets Compact.
- Carbon markets work involving blockchain or tokenization.
- Work focused on improving ESG ratings or market perception of a specific institution as the goal, rather than as an outcome.
- ESG portfolio strategy work.
- Work for consortia, associations, joint ventures, or other groups of competitors that are lawfully collaborating in the market.
- Financial crimes work involving sanctions or fraud remediation, except for lower-risk operational remediation efforts.
- Third-party reviews of practices, policies, or processes for a board or similar body where there is public scrutiny, a regulatory inquiry, or litigation.

Medium-risk work requires PRACL approval with input from other relevant cell leaders. Examples include:

- Work for state-owned, controlled, or linked banks.
- Work for early-stage fintech companies or new business builds where the conditions for impact may not be clear.
- Work for regulators intended to be used as input for drafting policy or regulation.
- Third-party reviews of practices, policies, or processes in connection with a regulatory inquiry.
- Compensation-related work involving a sales force that sells to customers or small businesses.
- Work related to capital and liquidity accuracy.
- Developing or validating climate risk models, assisting with regulatory ESG disclosures, or climate strategy work. Sustainability leadership approval is also required where applicable.
- Regulatory remediation programs or regulatory-related work that is not otherwise higher risk.
- Development or validation of sensitive models, including credit models for subprime segments, AML, CCAR, or AI models, for banking organizations supervised by regulators or financial institutions under consumer protection regulators.
- Retail pricing or sales enhancement work for retail products that could create misconduct risk, such as overcharging or abusive pricing structures.
- Pricing work for central banks, government-run entities, or consortia-run entities.
- Collections work that is not high risk because it does not primarily affect vulnerable populations.
- Work that includes or could lead to material staff reductions in audit, risk, compliance, or regulatory functions.
- Design, build, or implementation of cybersecurity work for financial institutions.
- Operational arrangements involving performance-based fees, fees at risk, or equity arrangements.
- New engagements entered into the system as confidential.

Teams should consult the relevant compacts, CITIO framework, sustainability guidance, technology delivery protocols, and risk leaders before proceeding with banking work that may fall into these categories.
