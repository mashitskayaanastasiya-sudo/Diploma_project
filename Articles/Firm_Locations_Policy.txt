Firm Locations Policy

This policy applies globally whenever a Firm location or Firm entity opening or closure is proposed.

The Client Committee must approve the opening of new Firm locations, including through acquisitions, when the proposed location will have client-facing professionals in a country where the Firm does not already have a client-serving location or office.

The Client Committee must also approve the closure of the last Firm location or office with client-facing professionals in a country.

Other location openings or closures are approved by the regional leader with sign-off from the chief financial officer, chief risk officer, and general counsel.

For new locations, the approval requirements are:

- New client-serving location in a new country: Client Committee approval.
- Adding client-facing professionals for the first time to an existing Firm location in a country with no other client-serving location: Client Committee approval.
- New location without client-facing professionals: regional leader approval with required sign-offs.
- New physical client-serving location in a country where the Firm already has a client-serving location: regional leader approval with required sign-offs.
- Legal, tax, or permanent establishment presence in any country: regional leader approval with required sign-offs.

The formation of any new Firm entity owned in whole or in part by the Firm, including joint ventures, requires sign-off from the chief financial officer, chief risk officer, and general counsel.

Before preparing a detailed risk assessment for a proposed new country presence, the sponsor must consider threshold questions, including:

- What client impact opportunity would the new location create?
- How does the location fit the regional client service strategy?
- What people model and economic model would apply?
- Would the Firm be permitted to conduct the full intended scope of work under applicable regulations and sanctions?
- Would the Firm be permitted to own any required new company, or would local third-party owners be required?
- Could the Firm ensure the safety of its people in line with international-company best practices?
- Would local employment requirements affect the people model, immigration approach, or start-up operations?
- Would foreign exchange barriers prevent money from flowing in or out of the location?

If the relevant regional leader, regional finance leader, Firm Legal, and Firm Security sign off on threshold considerations, a risk assessment team is convened.

The risk assessment should consider input from relevant Firm functions, including:

- Finance, for project coordination and preparation of the risk assessment document.
- Security, for security and healthcare risks and safeguards.
- Communications, for reputational risks, local stakeholder sensitivities, media monitoring, and communications strategy.
- Legal and Tax, for corporate structure, governance, liability, name protection, employment, immigration, public procurement, and tax implications.
- Benefits, for benefits law requirements.
- Real Estate, for real estate law requirements.
- Treasury, for foreign exchange, expatriation of funds, capitalization, invoice settlement, and banking arrangements.
- Controller, for internal account setup.
- Mobility, for mobility structures, immigration coordination, and compensation policy considerations.
- IT, for location-specific IT risks and support needs.

After the risk assessment, the sponsor should consider the risk assessment output and the client impact opportunity.

Before bringing a proposal to the Client Committee, the sponsor must:

- Develop a detailed financial plan.
- Obtain sign-off from the chief financial officer.
- Obtain sign-off from the relevant regional leader.
- Obtain sign-off from the chief risk officer.
- Obtain sign-off from the chair of the Client Committee.

For closures, the Client Committee approves the closure of the last Firm location with client-facing professionals in a country. The proposal should address relevant risk assessment and proposal considerations and requires prior sign-off from the regional leader, chief financial officer, chief risk officer, and general counsel.

The regional leader, with required sign-offs, approves closure of other Firm locations, including locations in countries with other client-serving locations and locations without client-facing professionals.

Closure, dissolution, or liquidation of any Firm entity owned in whole or in part by the Firm requires sign-off from the chief financial officer, chief risk officer, and general counsel.

Firm members must comply with this policy, act with integrity, and report violations. If local law is stricter, Firm members must follow local law.

The Firm does not tolerate retaliation for good-faith reporting or participation in a review or investigation.

Failure to comply may result in disciplinary action, including separation from the Firm. For third parties engaged by the Firm, violations may result in consequences up to and including termination of arrangements.
