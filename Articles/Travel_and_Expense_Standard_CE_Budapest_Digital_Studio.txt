Travel and Expense Rules and Thresholds Standard - Budapest Digital Studio

This standard supplements and is enforceable under the Travel and Expense Policy. It applies to Firm members assigned to the Budapest Digital Studio.

Only actual incurred expenses are reimbursable. Pre-paid value cards, estimated expenses, and per diem expenses are not reimbursable unless specifically allowed by this standard.

Hotel bills must be submitted with claims for hotel expenses and addressed to the COMPANY office address.

Receipts and invoices are required for all expenses. Cash payments require receipts showing who was paid and the city and country where payment was made. Slips from payment terminals are not accepted. Expenses in Hungary require a proper VAT invoice issued to the company name.

Expense reimbursement status or data update queries should be submitted through MyFinance.

The expense submission deadline is 60 days after the expense occurred. Reimbursement currency is HUF.

Where the corporate card is not accepted, Firm members may use a personal payment method and, if necessary, cash.

Firm members are responsible for managing corporate card accounts and making payments. Missing or delayed payments may lead to disciplinary measures, including termination, and other legal liabilities. The corporate card must not be used for personal expenses.

When travel is required, Firm members should make sustainable choices to reduce environmental impact.

Flight arrangements should be booked through the CE travel hub using Neo, phone, or email. For multi-city itineraries or travel within 24 hours, contact the travel team directly.

Firm Preferred Fare rules:

- Economy: the lowest logical fare must be booked.
- Business: allowed within the local financial threshold and time window.
- Financial thresholds assume a return fare; one-way thresholds are 50 percent of the return threshold.

Default air booking class:

- Client-related travel: Business class may be available when flight duration and weekly flight time meet the threshold.
- Non-client travel: Economy class is the default. Travelers should intend to book at least two weeks in advance using restricted or non-refundable tickets.
- Business class for non-client flights of at least four hours may be permitted with budget owner approval.
- Event, internal meeting, or learning-program travel guidelines supersede this standard when applicable.

Business class thresholds are based on total flight time. Layover time is excluded. If a non-stop flight is available, the cabin allowed on the non-stop flight determines eligibility.

Default rail booking class is Economy. Business class rail can be booked if the cost is lower than economy air travel for the same itinerary.

Accommodation requirements:

- Firm members must use preferred hotels where available and book through Firm Travel.
- Accommodation costs in the home city are not reimbursable.
- If no preferred hotel is available or suitable, travel service will offer a hotel within the city's maximum allowed rate.
- Extended stay or corporate apartments may be offered by Firm travel service. Airbnb is not permitted.
- Extended stays or corporate apartments over one month require applicable approval and must cost less than hotel accommodation for business nights.
- Hotel invoices should list the COMPANY home office address, not a personal home address.
- No-show charges are not reimbursable except in exceptional circumstances.

Other hotel services:

- Laundry is reimbursable within the daily threshold for trips longer than two nights.
- Hotel phones are reimbursable only if other options are unavailable.
- Pay TV, gyms outside the hotel, and spa treatments are not reimbursable.
- Minibar and room service are reimbursable within daily meal limits, but minibar alcohol is not reimbursable.

Ground transportation requirements:

- Use good judgment and professional standards when ordering taxis.
- Taxi expenses are covered only for business purposes. Daily commuting between home and office is not a business reason.
- Use client charge codes for business taxi expenses rather than office charge codes.
- Share ground transportation where reasonable.
- Non-premium ride hail services, taxis, or public transportation are preferred where practicable.
- Personal or rental cars may be used where other options are not practicable.

Monthly Budapest or Pest County public transport passes may be reimbursable for eligible colleagues. The relevant study or team charge code should be used.

Rental cars must be booked through Travel service. Rental cars are not allowed for personal use, including non-business weekend use. Mid-size cars are the standard; upgrades to specialty or luxury vehicles are not reimbursable.

Personal car expenses may be reimbursable for business mileage and fuel costs, subject to documentation requirements.

Parking during a business trip is reimbursable. Airport parking is reimbursable if it costs less than taxi transportation to and from the airport. Parking at the assigned COMPANY location and parking fines are not reimbursable. Business tolls are reimbursable.

Daily commuting or weekend travel home may be reimbursable if the permanent residential address is outside Budapest, subject to local caps and frequency limits.

Meal requirements:

- Individual meals in Hungary on a non-client code are reimbursable up to the local daily limit on days worked.
- Higher limits may apply for medically required restricted diets with appropriate documentation.
- Client-code meals have separate per-person limits depending on whether COMPANY staff only or clients attend.
- Individual meals on non-client and client codes cannot be combined within the country.
- Wolt credits should be used only for items that can be considered lunch.
- Groceries, alcohol, hygiene products, flowers, chocolate, cakes, desserts, and duty-free purchases are examples of inappropriate use of the allowance.
- Individual meals outside the home city follow country category limits.
- Team meals and team events have per-person limits and expected spacing between events.
- Group meals with non-Firm members require Anti-Corruption review and any necessary approvals before the expense is incurred.
- Tips are reimbursable only if reasonable, customary, included in the receipt, and not handwritten.

Weekend travel frequency depends on geography and direct flight time. Staying in the work city for the weekend may allow hotel and meal reimbursement up to the value of returning home.

Spouse or significant other travel may be allowed at specified frequency, but reimbursement is a taxable benefit-in-kind and must be booked by the Firm's travel service with required notifications and approvals.

Leveraged fly-out travel to a non-home city may be allowed if the airfare does not exceed the allowed home-city fare comparison. Other expenses for the non-home weekend stay are personal.

Events over the local equivalent of US$20,000 that require an overnight stay must be registered through Optimize Events.

Internal gifts for eligible occasions must not exceed the local per-person limit and should be arranged through Office Services. Gifts outside the policy, including birthday cake and gift cards or vouchers, are not reimbursable.

External gifts must comply with the Anti-Corruption Policy. Government official gifts above the threshold and non-government official gifts above the threshold require preapproval.

Other expenses:

- Loaner mobile devices can be obtained through GHD; personally sourced telecom devices or service are not reimbursable.
- Home phone and internet are not reimbursable.
- IT supplies require local IT coordinator approval.
- Professional publications and market research require practice knowledge team approval.
- Professional membership fees and ongoing education fees are generally not reimbursable unless necessary for the role and handled through the office.
- Office supplies should be obtained from office services where possible.
- Conferences and networking expenses should be paid by the office directly rather than expensed.
- Business visa fees are reimbursable.
- Passport renewal due to expiration is personal; renewal due to insufficient pages from Firm travel is reimbursable.
- Engagement-related delivery and postage are reimbursable.
- Corporate or credit card late fees are not reimbursable.
- Business-related bank currency fees may be reimbursable with documentation.
- In-flight Wi-Fi is reimbursable if client-related or on long-haul flights.
- Required travel immunizations may be reimbursable if not covered by health insurance.
- Lost items are not reimbursable.

Before engaging a new supplier, check whether the supplier already exists in Aravo. If not, submit a request for risk-based due diligence and onboarding. Supplier risk should be managed in line with the Sustainable Procurement and Responsible Buying Policy.

Exceptions are expected to be rare and require written approval from a Partner for client-related matters or the Location Manager for non-client matters.

Travel safety requirements should follow the Travel Safety Standard. If a Firm member becomes sick while traveling on Firm business, they should contact the Firm's travel medical assistance company or their medical plan provider.

Travel to Restricted Jurisdictions may be approved only case by case. Carrying items beyond personal luggage across countries may require advance authorization or customs payments.
