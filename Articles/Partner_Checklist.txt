Partner Checklist

This checklist is a practical reference for partners. It summarizes selected client service and Firm requirements and is not exhaustive. Partners remain responsible for understanding and complying with all applicable Firm policies, standards, procedures, and local requirements.

Partners should pay particular attention to requirements related to client engagement lifecycle management, ongoing partner activities, client interactions, Firm operations, communications, conflicts, data management, and expense processes.

Key partner actions include:

- Obtain required pre-approvals for gifts, meals, entertainment, and travel.
- Flag changes to work in progress with Finance when required.
- Report allegations, violations, or suspected violations through the appropriate channels.
- Pre-approve invitations to government officials when thresholds or risk requirements apply.
- Report personal conflicts when they arise.
- Retain Firm business communications outside approved applications when retention requirements apply.
- Escalate unauthorized client data transfers or disclosures to the Security Operations Center.
- Obtain required approvals for publications from affected cell leaders.
- Submit timesheets and expenses accurately and on time.

Before an engagement begins, partners should:

- Register the engagement in Engage as early as possible.
- Complete the engagement risk assessment.
- Review applicable sector risk guidance.
- Initiate required client diligence.
- Upload required legal documents.
- Obtain assigned senior partner approval when required.
- Monitor required risk reviews.
- Confirm that diligence, conflicts, and risk reviews are complete.
- Obtain enhanced reviews for higher-risk engagements when required.
- Obtain required approvals from the relationship service leader, office managing partner, client service risk committee, practice risk approvers, or other approval bodies when required.
- Confirm and implement required guardrails.

During and after an engagement, partners should:

- Brief the client service team on applicable guardrails.
- Monitor compliance with required guardrails and risk conditions.
- Pause work and contact the Risk Advisor when there is a material change, guardrail violation, policy violation, or heightened risk.
- Submit final documents and required close-down documentation within required timelines.
- Verify that engagement materials are complete and properly handled.

Certain engagement types may require additional steps, including technology delivery, mergers and acquisitions, client-facing collaborators, external workers, advisors, and senior advisors.

For these engagement types, partners should:

- Register the work in the required systems.
- Review and complete required contracts.
- Confirm required trainings.
- Obtain required approvals.
- Follow any additional guardrails or procedures that apply.

Partners should also manage ongoing responsibilities related to:

- Gifts, meals, entertainment, and travel.
- Client events.
- Business communications.
- Publications.
- Charitable donations.
- Responsible writing.
- Client finance.
- Ethics and compliance.
- Recruiting and hiring.
- Data management.
- Timesheets and expenses.
