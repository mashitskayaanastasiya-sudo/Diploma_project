Compact Global SHaPE Practice HSS

This compact identifies HSS engagement topics that are not approved, require CSRC approval, require RSB approval, or require PRACL approval with input from relevant leaders.

Work that is not approved includes:

- Balance billing expansion, typically in North America but not limited to North America.
- Real or perceived clinical decision-making.
- Actuarial certification.
- Policy or regulatory advocacy.
- Support for initiatives that make it more difficult to qualify for charity care.
- Pricing work that is anti-competitive.
- Transaction-level analytics to define provider charge masters.
- Work focused on addictive substances or individuals experiencing addiction, including opioids, antipsychotics, or other addictive substances.
- Consumer sales or marketing.
- Patient or consumer collections.
- Clinical coding accuracy.

Clinical decision-making means evaluating and interpreting medical information, including patient history, physical exam findings, or diagnostic information, by a licensed physician, advanced practice provider, or allied health professional who is permitted to make diagnostic and treatment decisions and develop a treatment plan.

Patient refers to individuals with established relationships with a provider. Consumer refers to patients or prospective patients.

Vulnerable individuals include racial or ethnic minorities, children, elderly people, socioeconomically disadvantaged people, underinsured people, people with certain medical conditions, Medicare and Medicaid populations, and indigenous populations.

High-risk work requires CSRC approval with input from PRACL and other relevant cell leaders, as well as ongoing deliverable review. Examples include:

- Analytics to support medical and pharmacy policy or formulary decisions for health insurers, including PBMs.
- Reimbursement or pricing analytics in a common-market merger or roll-up context.
- Healthcare and fintech crossover work, including medical credit cards or other forms of healthcare financing for patients.
- Digital tools that help patients with clinical or care selection.
- Charge master accuracy.
- Closure of acute or emergency care delivery sites, such as hospitals or emergency departments.
- At-risk arrangements outside pre-approved frameworks.
- Fact-based support for regulatory advocacy or policy making.
- Work that disproportionately affects vulnerable patients.

Medium-risk plus work requires RSB approval with input from PRACL and other relevant cell leaders, with the option to escalate to CSRC. Examples include:

- Referral management.
- Utilization management or payment integrity.

Medium-risk work requires PRACL approval with input from other relevant cell leaders, with the option to escalate to CSRC. Examples include:

- Hospital operations involving clinical staffing ratios, operating room turnaround times, or device selection.
- Pricing.
- Analytics involving PHI or PII data.
- Combining disparate data sets at an individual level.
- Work involving care models, including care management, care pathways, case management, patient segmentation, care gap analysis, and clinical quality.
- Engagements related to mental health services, consistent with the Behavioral Health Center of Excellence framework.
- Any work related to the 340B program.

Questions involving addictive substances or the interpretation of related restrictions should be escalated to Client Service Risk and Legal.
