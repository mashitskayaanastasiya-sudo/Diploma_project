Anti-Corruption Guidelines for Charitable Donations and Charitable Sponsorships

The Firm supports charitable giving and community involvement, but charitable donations and charitable sponsorships made on behalf of the Firm must be handled carefully. They must be used for their intended purpose and must not be construed as bribes or improper payments.

Firm members coordinating a charitable donation on behalf of the Firm must follow the required guidance before committing to the donation and must ensure anti-corruption pre-clearance has been obtained where required.

Categories that require charitable giving anti-corruption pre-clearance include:

- Monetary donations using Firm funds.
- In-kind contributions of goods, equipment, or facilities.
- Firm grants donations.
- Charitable sponsorships.
- Fellowships.
- Charitable volunteering activities, including Firm volunteer programs.

Before requesting pre-clearance, the Firm member should research the charitable organization and confirm that:

- The organization has a social impact mission.
- The organization is duly registered in compliance with local law.
- The organization will not expose the Firm to potential or actual reputational damage.
- The donation is not made to a prohibited recipient.
- The donation is not made for a prohibited purpose.

Charitable donations must:

- Not be made in cash, cash equivalents, or purchasing cards.
- Be documented accurately in the Firm's records.
- Be properly receipted.
- Comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and Firm policies.

To request pre-clearance, Firm members must submit the request through the charitable giving anti-corruption pre-clearance process.

If the request is rejected, the charitable donation cannot be made.

If the request is pre-cleared, the pre-clearance process sends a confirmation. The Firm member must then obtain any additional pre-approvals listed in the confirmation, such as approval from the location managing partner when required.

After receiving pre-clearance, the Firm member should:

- Submit the pre-clearance confirmation to Finance for payment processing.
- Ensure the recipient organization is onboarded through the required supplier process if needed.
- Complete the charitable donation letter template to accompany the payment.
- Charge the donation to the commodity code for gifts, awards, and charity.
- Not charge the donation to a client WIP or client charge code.

North America-based Firm members should contact the North America charitable contributions team before completing the pre-clearance form. Other geographies should follow the general process.

Pre-clearance is not required for:

- Pro bono engagements.
- Zero-fee engagements with government entities.
- Donations made through the Firm matching program on the designated platform.
- Non-charitable sponsorships or memberships.
- Non-profit board membership fees.
- Natural disaster donations of goods, such as clothing, water, food, or hygiene products, made to Red Cross, Red Crescent, or a government entity addressing the disaster.

Bribes can take many forms, including charitable donations and charitable sponsorships. Interactions with government officials present elevated risk, and Firm members must identify and avoid situations that could violate anti-corruption laws.

Firm members must immediately report concerns if they suspect a charitable donation or charitable sponsorship:

- May be made for an improper purpose.
- May involve a recipient making improper payments to government officials.
- May involve conduct against Firm values, Firm policies, the Code of Conduct, or applicable law.

Prohibited recipients include:

- Individuals.
- Organizations whose primary purpose is to promote political views, influence legislation, or support candidates for public office.
- Organizations located in restricted jurisdictions.
- Sanctioned parties.
- Non-profits that do not hold required exempt status, such as 501(c)(3) status in the United States or an equivalent outside the United States.

Prohibited purposes include:

- Being or creating the appearance of a bribe, kickback, or other wrongful conduct.
- Directly or indirectly procuring future Firm business.
- Improperly obtaining or retaining business or official government action.
- Securing an improper advantage.
- Inducing anyone to act unfairly.
- Circumventing the Anti-Corruption Procedure on Gifts, Meals, Entertainment, and Travel.
- Causing the Firm to be involved in sanctionable activities.

Pre-clearance is required for each charitable donation or sponsorship instance. A prior year's sponsorship does not automatically cover a new request.

If a speaker requests a charitable donation instead of a speaker payment, the Firm member must request pre-clearance before committing the Firm to make the donation.

Charitable donation invoices are paid through Finance Accounts Payable. Donations should not be charged to a corporate card, made in cash, made with cash equivalents, or paid by purchasing card.

The typical pre-clearance review takes three to five business days. Review may take longer, including up to 14 business days, when additional review is triggered.
