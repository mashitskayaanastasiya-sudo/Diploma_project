Standard on Clinicians Maintaining Clinical Hours

Medical practitioner colleagues may need to participate in clinical hours without taking a leave of absence. Regular clinical participation can help colleagues stay current with medical trends and knowledge and may be important to SHaPE and Life Sciences clients.

Clinical Time means delivery of patient care, including medical and ancillary services, by an appropriately licensed medical, nursing, or allied health professional.

SHaPE or Life Sciences colleagues who are licensed medical practitioners may participate in Clinical Time without taking a Leave of Absence or Take Time if they meet the required conditions.

To participate in Clinical Time, the Firm member must:

- Secure PD or practice leadership support.
- Ensure appropriate medical malpractice insurance is carried by the provider for whom services are performed or by the colleague.
- Avoid mixing clinical duties with Firm duties.
- Apply client conflict principles.
- Avoid using Firm email or resources for clinical work.
- Avoid scheduling clinical hours while on a study where demands can be unexpected.
- Have Firm Legal review any employment contract to confirm it does not create Firm conflicts.
- Clearly record on timesheets whether a day is spent partly on Firm duties and partly on clinical duties.
- Use PTO for occasional full or partial day Clinical Time.
- Recognize that PTO is not required for Clinical Time performed at night or on weekends.
- Consider moving to a part-time COMPANY schedule for regular clinical hours.
- Donate Clinical Time compensation if on a 100 percent schedule above the Firm-approved three days a month of paid clinical time.

Reduced hours may allow receipt of compensation. Colleagues using Take Time remain eligible to be paid for Clinical Time.

The Firm will support up to three days a month of paid clinical work, aggregated over 12 months, because clinical skills can contribute to client impact. The colleague should charge these days to the HR-provided charge code so time can be tracked.

If a client service team is serving a healthcare company that also employs a COMPANY healthcare professional for clinical time, the statement of work should consider a disclaimer explaining that the clinical work is under a separate arrangement, distinct from COMPANY services, and does not constitute clinical, medical, or regulated advice from COMPANY.

The Accountability provisions of the Outside Activities Policy apply.
