Personal Investments Policy - Popular Topics

Firm members are not required to sell single-name publicly traded securities they already own. However, trading in single-name publicly traded securities is prohibited for the duration of employment at the Firm unless Compliance grants a documented exception.

Firm members may serve a public entity client while holding securities in that company if the securities were purchased in line with Firm policy or before joining the Firm. Firm members may hold those securities, but may not sell them while employed by the Firm unless an exception is approved.

Permitted investments and accounts include:

- Mutual funds.
- Index funds.
- Exchange-traded funds, excluding single-stock ETFs.
- Money market funds.
- United States Section 529 college savings and prepaid tuition plans.
- National government securities.
- Accounts managed by an unrelated professional advisor or broker with full discretionary trading authority.
- Certain trades executed for unrelated third parties by household members who work for a broker-dealer or investment adviser and are subject to that employer's insider trading policy.
- Household members' trades in their employer's securities or options under that employer's compensation, stock option, or employee stock purchase plans.
- Irrevocable pre-established trading plans or orders that were in place before the policy was implemented.

Firm members may not share the full Personal Investments Policy with financial advisors. They may explain that trading through a professional advisor or broker is permitted only if:

- The advisor or broker is unrelated to the Firm member.
- The advisor or broker has written instructions to trade without consultation about specific securities.
- The advisor or broker has complete discretion to initiate and complete trades.
- The Firm member or covered household member cannot direct, approve, or request trades in specific securities.
- Communications are limited to trade confirmations, account statements, fee discussions, and general investment objectives, strategies, or risk tolerance.
- The Firm member or covered household member does not suggest or communicate about specific companies or securities in advance.

Transactions in currencies, cryptocurrencies, and non-fungible tokens are permitted if the Firm member does not use Firm or client confidential information. Currency and NFT trading does not require an exception request.

If a cryptoasset is a security, an exception request is required. The Firm does not currently consider Bitcoin, Ethereum, Bitcoin SV, Bitcoin Cash, Dogecoin, Litecoin, or Ethereum Classic to be securities. For other cryptoassets, Firm members are responsible for determining whether the asset is a security.

The policy applies to Firm members and covered household members. Covered household members include spouses, domestic partners, and dependents who live in the Firm member's home for the majority of the year.

New hires have a 30-day grace period to sell publicly traded securities they already own without preclearance.

Trades by a spouse or household member in their employer's stock or stock options do not need to be pre-cleared if made under the employer's policies and procedures.

Private equity fund investments may be acceptable if they are passive and do not create conflicts with Firm or client responsibilities. Firm members may not:

- Use the Firm's name or resources for investment purposes.
- Make investments that conflict with Firm or client responsibilities.
- Use client or Firm relationships to support the investment.
- Engage in investment activity that negatively affects Firm commitments or creates reputational risk.
- Use Firm or client confidential information, including material nonpublic information, for investment purposes.
- Disclose material nonpublic information or confidential Firm or client information to others.
- Recommend the purchase or sale of a security while in possession of material nonpublic information.

Investments in private entities must be passive. The opportunity should not have been learned of through a client or Firm situation. Firm members may not serve a private entity client while holding any investment in that client or its affiliates.

Firm members must promptly raise personal conflicts to the accountable senior partner, staffing personnel, or their supervisor when a personal situation may create an actual or perceived conflict.

Private equity and hedge fund investments must also meet these requirements:

- The Firm member has no input or control over the investment mix.
- The investment is made in the fund, and the fund determines how money is invested.
- The Firm member is not aware before investing that any client they serve is part of the investment mix.
- If the Firm member later learns that the fund invested in a client they serve, they must raise the matter to the PIP Team for review and may need to stop serving the client or exit the investment when possible.
