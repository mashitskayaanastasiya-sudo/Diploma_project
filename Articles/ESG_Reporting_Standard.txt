ESG Reporting Standard

This standard supports the Firm in complying with environmental, social, and governance regulations and standards. It sets requirements for mandatory and voluntary ESG reporting.

The standard aims to ensure that ESG reporting is accurate, reliable, and consistent with relevant regulations, standards, and frameworks.

The standard applies globally to Firm offices, practices, functions, and individuals responsible for mandatory or voluntary reporting on ESG topics. These responsible parties are report owners.

The standard also applies to Firm functions that support report owners by providing data or conducting reviews needed for ESG reporting.

ESG reporting reflects the Firm's policies, programs, and performance on ESG topics across global operations or specific locations, depending on reporting scope.

ESG reporting includes mandatory and voluntary reporting on:

- Environmental topics, such as climate footprint, water, waste, and natural resource impact.
- Social topics, such as employees, clients, suppliers, communities, stakeholders, human rights, working conditions, and pay equity.
- Governance topics, such as Firm governance, ethics and compliance, client service diligence, and risk management.

Reporting under this standard is different from external marketing communications on the Firm website.

Additional reporting requirements on topics such as artificial intelligence, cyber, data privacy, and taxation are excluded from this standard. Firm Legal or Ethics and Compliance should be consulted if it is unclear whether a topic is in scope.

The Firm is committed to complying with applicable laws in all jurisdictions where it operates and to upholding voluntary ESG reporting principles in which it participates.

Report owners and supporting Firm functions must comply with applicable regulations, disclosure standards, and industry frameworks at global and local levels.

The Firm must:

- Monitor new regulations and updates to existing regulations.
- Assess legal requirements.
- Determine reporting scope.
- Ensure compliance with evolving requirements.

Externally reported ESG information must be:

- Accurate.
- Relevant.
- Complete.
- Comparable.
- Verifiable.

Report owners must follow a standard ESG reporting process that includes:

- Developing a reporting plan.
- Conducting materiality and risk assessments where applicable.
- Collecting, validating, and certifying data through a standardized process.
- Drafting and reviewing reports according to applicable requirements.
- Obtaining legal advice from Firm Legal and outside counsel.
- Obtaining approvals and sign-offs from relevant subject matter experts and governing bodies where applicable.
- Engaging external assurance providers when required by regulation.
- Submitting reports to regulatory bodies by the reporting deadlines.
- Publishing reports on the website when required.

The scope of data validation and certification may differ for voluntary and mandatory reporting.

Firm members must comply with this standard, act with integrity, and report violations. If local law is stricter, Firm members must follow local law.

The Firm does not tolerate retaliation for good-faith reporting or participation in a review or investigation.

Failure to comply may result in disciplinary action, including separation from the Firm. For third parties engaged by the Firm, violations may result in consequences up to and including termination of the arrangement.
