Social Media Guidelines

Social media is an important communication channel for clients, colleagues, and the Firm. Firm members are expected to act professionally when engaging in professional or personal conversations, building knowledge and networks, or staying in touch with colleagues and friends.

Firm members must uphold confidentiality and professionalism in all settings, including social media.

These guidelines apply to websites and applications including:

- Social media networks such as LinkedIn and Facebook.
- Blogs such as WordPress, Medium, and Tumblr.
- Public microblogs such as Twitter and WeChat.
- Forums and wikis such as Quora and Wikipedia.
- Video and photo sharing sites such as Instagram, YouTube, and Snapchat.

Three principles guide social media participation:

- Maintain client and Firm confidentiality.
- Enhance and protect your reputation and the Firm's reputation.
- Uphold Firm values and policies.

These principles apply when acting as a designated spokesperson, discussing professional topics, or using social media personally.

Confidentiality online is especially important because information can be public and permanent. Do not post proprietary COMPANY knowledge or confidential information about clients, colleagues, or the Firm.

Examples of confidentiality safeguards:

- If a client is identifiable from a location, do not check in on social media, enable location services, or post until after leaving the area.
- Do not include logos, signage, or other details in photos that could identify a client site.
- Examine photos before posting for visual identifying information, including prominent executives.
- Do not share data from research reports purchased by the Firm.
- Do not share insights from client engagements.
- LinkedIn and other profiles should not reveal information that allows third parties to identify current or previous clients.
- Do not accept many invitations from the same client company all at once if that could reveal client relationships.
- Consider privacy settings so only you can see your connections.
- Proprietary COMPANY knowledge, confidential data, and client discussions must occur through secure Firm or client infrastructure.
- Do not conduct ongoing client conversations through LinkedIn, private email, WhatsApp, WeChat, or similar channels.

Social media activity is on the record and can be permanent. Before posting, consider the impact on your reputation and the Firm's reputation. Clients may search online for team members, and recruits may search for Firm members they meet.

If you identify the Firm as your employer and express personal views, include a disclaimer stating that the views are yours and not those of your employer.

Communications should be measured, constructive, and balanced. Assume anything posted may be copied elsewhere or taken out of context.

Local posts can have wider significance. Supporting or endorsing a local business leader or initiative may have implications beyond the local community.

Publishing or contributing to a blog on topics related to Firm client work may require syndication and approval under the Publications and External Communications in a Personal Capacity Policy.

Only designated and trained Firm spokespersons may speak on behalf of a cell or practice. Anyone speaking on behalf of a cell or practice should be transparent and disclose their Firm affiliation.

Firm members should manage privacy settings, use strong passwords, and enable two-factor authentication.

To manage online profile risk:

- Monitor posts that reference or relate to you.
- Conduct periodic online searches of yourself.
- Review connections periodically to ensure you know and are comfortable with who is connected to your profile.

Firm members must comply with all Firm policies when using social media, including policies on external communications, use of name, information sharing, IT security, and serving competitors.

Be respectful of colleagues, public figures, businesses, institutions, and individuals. Do not misquote or misrepresent people or organizations. Online comments may reflect on you and may be seen as reflecting on the Firm.

Misuse of social media can negatively affect the Firm, clients, and colleagues, and may violate obligations to the Firm.

Authorized Firm personnel may review, monitor, or act on information made available online, including public social media activity, subject to safeguards and applicable law.

Clients, vendors, and advisors may not mention the Firm publicly, including on social media, without permission. Any use of the Firm name should be discussed with External Relations or the Social Media team.

If you see inappropriate mentions of the Firm by a client, vendor, or advisor, contact External Relations or the Social Media team.

If you see online content about COMPANY or Firm work that may warrant attention, do not respond directly. Contact the appropriate Firm contact or a partner or cell leader.

The Firm's work can make Firm members targets for people trying to obtain information. When connecting on LinkedIn, confirm COMPANY colleagues through approved Firm directories. If someone appears to be impersonating a Firm member, report it.

These guidelines are not intended to restrict or interfere with employee rights.
