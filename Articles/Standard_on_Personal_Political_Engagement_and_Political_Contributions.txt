Standard on Personal Political Engagement and Political Contributions

This standard supplements the Interactions with Government Officials and Lobbying Policy. Firm members must also comply with all laws governing political engagement and political contributions in their jurisdictions.

The standard applies globally to all Firm members, subject to applicable law.

Firm members may not represent their personal political affiliations or views as those of the Firm.

Partisan political activities, such as volunteering for a political campaign, must be undertaken in a personal capacity and outside working hours.

Firm members may not use Volunteer Time Off to volunteer with government entities, political organizations, or political campaigns.

Firm members may not use the Firm's name or resources for political purposes. Firm resources include:

- Email addresses.
- Mailing lists.
- Printers and copiers.
- Office supplies.
- Equipment.
- Facilities.
- Corporate logos.
- Intellectual property.
- Other Firm personnel.

Firm members may not engage in activity that creates the appearance that the Firm supports, endorses, or opposes a political candidate, party, election outcome, ballot measure, or similar referendum.

Firm members must also follow the Outside Activities Policy, which prohibits certain types of political involvement.

The standard does not prohibit use of Firm resources to support the Firm's United States political action committee or eligible Firm members from supporting that committee in accordance with applicable law.

Political contributions by Firm members must:

- Be made with personal funds.
- Comply with applicable campaign finance and pay-to-play laws.
- Not be submitted for reimbursement.
- Not be charged to a Firm corporate card, even if later paid with personal funds.

Firm members may not make political contributions directly or indirectly in the Firm's name or on the Firm's behalf. This prohibition applies to cash and in-kind contributions of goods or services to:

- Public officeholders.
- Candidates for office.
- Campaign committees.
- Political parties.
- Political action committees.

Any exception must be pre-approved in writing by Public Affairs and Ethics and Compliance.

Special requirements apply to certain United States citizens or green card holders, including:

- Officers, directors, and board members of specified Firm entities.
- Senior partners.
- Individuals in Firm leadership roles.
- Firm members involved in marketing to or soliciting business from state or local government entities.
- Firm members involved in preparing responses to state or local government RFPs or bids.

These individuals are covered colleagues. If it is unclear whether a Firm member is a covered colleague, they should contact Public Affairs and Ethics and Compliance.

Because the Firm is an active and prospective government contractor, political contributions by covered colleagues and their immediate family members may be restricted in certain United States states and localities.

A restricted contribution may make the Firm ineligible for government contracts or subject the Firm to penalties, including loss of work, fines, and debarment.

Covered colleagues must seek advance approval before making political contributions to:

- State or local officeholders.
- State or local candidates.
- State or local political parties.
- Political action committees that contribute to state or local candidates.

The advance approval requirement also applies to immediate family members of covered colleagues. Immediate family members include spouses, domestic partners, and dependent children who live in the covered colleague's home for the majority of the year.

Advance approval is not required for contributions to the Firm's United States political action committee.

Personal political contributions to United States federal candidates are generally not subject to these restrictions. However, if a current state or local official is running for federal office, contributions by covered colleagues or their immediate family members require pre-approval because state or local pay-to-play restrictions may still apply.

Firm members must comply with this standard, act with integrity, and report violations. If local law is stricter, Firm members must follow local law.

The Firm does not tolerate retaliation for good-faith reporting or participation in a review or investigation.

Failure to comply may result in disciplinary action, including separation from the Firm.
