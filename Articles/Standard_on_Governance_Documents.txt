Standard on Governance Documents

This standard applies to Firm governance documents, including policies, standards, procedures, and guidelines. Governance documents help describe mandatory requirements, recommended practices, and supporting processes across the Firm.

A policy sets mandatory Firm requirements. Policies should be clear, concise, and aligned with applicable legal, regulatory, professional, ethical, and Firm obligations.

A standard sets mandatory requirements that support or implement a policy. Standards should explain the specific rules, thresholds, roles, or processes needed to comply with the related policy.

A procedure explains how to complete a process or activity. Procedures may include step-by-step instructions, workflows, approvals, roles, or operational details.

A guideline provides recommended practices or interpretive guidance. Guidelines may help Firm members apply a policy, standard, or procedure in common situations.

Governance documents should be written using approved templates and should include clear ownership, scope, effective date, review cycle, and document control information.

Governance documents should be reviewed at least annually unless a shorter review cycle is required. Reviews should confirm that the document remains current, accurate, and aligned with Firm obligations and operating practices.

Records related to governance documents should be retained according to applicable recordkeeping requirements. Document owners should maintain appropriate evidence of approvals, material changes, exceptions, and review history.

New governance documents, material updates, and exceptions should follow the applicable approval process. Changes should be reviewed by the appropriate accountable parties before publication.

Firm members are expected to comply with applicable governance documents. Failure to comply may result in consequences consistent with Firm policies and local requirements.

Document owners are accountable for:

- Maintaining the governance document.
- Completing required reviews.
- Coordinating required approvals.
- Communicating material changes when needed.
- Ensuring the document remains consistent with related governance documents.

Annexes may define terms, clarify roles, or describe pre-approved exceptions. Annexes should be maintained with the same care as the main governance document.
