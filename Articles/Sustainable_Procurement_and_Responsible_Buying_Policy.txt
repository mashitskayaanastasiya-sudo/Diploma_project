Sustainable Procurement and Responsible Buying Policy

The Firm considers environmental and social impact across the lifecycle of goods and services it purchases, from sourcing practices and supplier selection through retirement or disposal of products and services.

The Firm complies with applicable social and environmental laws and regulations. Sustainable procurement practices support the Firm's public commitments to the UN Sustainable Development Goals, the Paris Climate Agreement, and the United Nations Global Compact.

The Firm is committed to working with suppliers to help improve the sustainability of their practices and the social and environmental impact of the goods and services they offer.

All suppliers must complete onboarding through the Firm's supplier portal before being engaged to provide a product or service. Suppliers inactive for at least 18 months are deactivated from payment systems and must repeat onboarding before future use.

Purchase approval thresholds based on total spend commitment promote transparency and accountability. To avoid conflicts of interest, the Firm limits client service team involvement in sourcing decisions and in use of Firm supplier contracts for client work.

This policy applies globally to all Firm members. Responsible buying requirements apply to all interactions with external entities that provide products or services to the Firm.

Firm members should contact Ethics & Compliance if they have questions about how this policy interacts with another policy.

Sustainable procurement requirements:

- Consider environmental and social impact across the lifecycle of goods and services.
- Respect human rights, including fair working conditions and wages.
- Oppose child labor, forced labor, exploited labor, and exploitative working conditions.
- Prohibit corruption, including kickbacks.
- Follow international trade regulations that prohibit or restrict trade with certain countries, organizations, or individuals.
- Perform environmental, human rights, labor, and business ethics due diligence on suppliers.
- Prioritize suppliers committed to reducing environmental footprint and increasing diversity and inclusion.
- Reduce supply-chain emissions and support Firm climate commitments.
- Reduce demand and environmental footprint by purchasing less, reusing where possible, and recycling at end of life.
- Use technology as an alternative to travel where appropriate.
- Choose less environmentally harmful travel options where travel is needed.
- Include products or services that minimize natural resource use or are sustainably produced.
- Work with qualified diverse suppliers where possible.

The Firm seeks to hold suppliers to high social responsibility and sustainability standards through the Supplier Code of Conduct. Implementation may be supported through supplier self-assessments, evidence-based assessments, or audits. ESG questions are included in Optimize-led RFPs.

Responsible buying means purchasing the optimal product or service for a confirmed business need in a way that mitigates risk and is sustainable and inclusive.

Before committing to a purchase with a supplier, Firm members must:

- Assess whether the supplier is appropriate, including reputation, quality of products or services, and pricing structure.
- Maintain documentation of the supplier assessment.
- Confirm that the supplier is approved in the Firm's supplier portal.
- Use existing approved suppliers wherever possible.
- If the supplier is not approved, submit it for onboarding through the supplier portal.
- Wait for onboarding confirmation before proceeding.
- Complete any additional requirements, such as a contract or cybersecurity vendor assessment where the supplier hosts, stores, or processes Firm or client data.
- Execute a contractual agreement, statement of work, or order form unless COMPANY Legal grants an exception.

Relationship owners who engage and onboard suppliers must:

- Support updates to supplier information when requested.
- Monitor invoices for accuracy and timely payment.
- Review invoices and supplier relationships for red flags.
- Ensure contracts, renewals, and offboarding are handled promptly.

Suppliers without spend for at least 18 months will be deactivated. Re-engaging a deactivated supplier requires new onboarding.

Purchase approval requirements:

- The Responsible Approver must be at the required impact level based on total spend commitment.
- For client charge codes, purchases up to the local threshold may be approved by an Engagement Manager; higher spend requires Responsible Partner approval.
- For non-client charge codes, approval levels increase with total spend commitment, up to Finance and Infrastructure Committee approval for the largest commitments.

Total spend commitment means:

- Committed spend over the term of the agreement, regardless of termination rights.
- If no committed spend exists, estimated variable spend over the agreement term.
- All purchases for the same project should be included, whether with one supplier or multiple suppliers.

Optimize must be engaged for any purchase with a total spend commitment of US$10 million or more. Optimize should be contacted before committing to the purchase.

Where Optimize leads or supports a purchase:

- Optimize owns supplier selection or award.
- Optimize determines whether to conduct a competitive bid.
- The process and award rationale are documented for purchases above US$500,000.
- Purchases above US$5 million annually with one supplier require a competitive bid unless the Chief Procurement Officer grants a written exception.

Supplier and client conflict requirements:

- The Firm does not provide preference in commercial dealings based only on an existing or potential client relationship.
- Client service teams may not influence purchasing decisions where their client is an existing or potential supplier.
- Client service teams must remove themselves from decisions about products or services where their clients are current or potential suppliers.
- Client service teams may not use Firm supplier contracts or contract details for client service, benchmarking, or similar purposes.
- Client service teams should not recommend Firm suppliers to clients or provide references for their work.

A Firm member who introduces a supplier must disclose any personal or financial interest or other relationship with the supplier during onboarding. If the supplier is approved, the Firm member may not manage the supplier relationship or participate in purchasing decisions, negotiations, invoice management, or payment.

Firm members must comply with this policy and applicable laws. Failure to comply may result in disciplinary action, including separation from the Firm.

Firm members may raise concerns about values, ethics, and professional conduct related to this policy without fear of retribution.
