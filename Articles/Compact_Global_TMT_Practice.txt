Compact Global TMT Practice

This compact applies to TMT work outside Greater China Region and identifies work that is not approved, high risk, medium-risk plus, or medium risk.

Work that is not approved includes:

- Go-to-market work for clients that are not authorized to operate in a given market, such as clients banned by a local government due to corruption.
- Work for TMT clients that can reasonably be understood to have a nexus to law enforcement, national security, military, or defense in a country with an EIU Democracy Index score below 6.
- Work touching tobacco products, including vaping, heat-not-burn, or nicotine, or supplying materials or services to the tobacco industry.
- Work requiring FARA registration with the U.S. Department of Justice.
- Direct content moderation for technology platforms where colleagues could be exposed to violent or damaging content.
- Work for pornography clients or business units on pornography topics.
- Certain gambling and sports betting work, including work in countries where gambling is unregulated or illegal, work that grows customers, usage, or loyalty for online-only gambling entities, brand repair, crisis response, or work restricted by other Firm policies.
- Use of the Firm name to audit or endorse platform content moderation or privacy programs.

High-risk work requires CSRC approval with input from PRACL and other relevant cell leaders, as well as ongoing deliverable review. Examples include:

- Site manufacturing decisions in geopolitically sensitive regions.
- New clients with more than 20% ownership by a controversial institution or by a government in a country with an EIU score below 6.
- Current clients with such ownership that recently experienced a significant change in ownership or control.
- Current clients with such ownership where the engagement would otherwise be medium risk under this compact.
- Clients in bankruptcy.
- Foreign companies with a local presence only for lobbying purposes.
- Consumer privacy work involving access to or collection of patient or user data for e-health engagements, such as smart watches or handsets, with lower risk possible in countries with EIU scores above 6.
- High-risk AI products, synthetic cloning, or robotics and automation affecting vulnerable job groups.
- Technology use cases designed for defense, police, or online gaming platforms, or with significant potential for abuse by bad actors, such as biometrics, image recognition, individual scoring technologies, or 5G.
- Spectrum auctions or work in regulated industries with regulatory or competitor conflict risk.

Medium-risk plus work requires CSRC approval with input from PRACL and other relevant cell leaders. Examples include:

- New gambling clients.
- Gambling and sports betting work requiring access to PII, building new businesses beyond the core, or expanding into new markets.
- Privacy issues, including collaborative assessment, design, and reporting of privacy practices.
- Smart cities.

Medium-risk work requires PRACL approval with input from other relevant cell leaders. Examples include:

- Gambling, sports betting, or engagements serving lotteries.
- Work touching cannabis products, including CBD, or supplying materials or services to the cannabis industry.
- Technology and telecommunications equipment manufacturers with potential government associations.
- Journalism or news outlets, where the risk of work becoming public is higher than usual.
- Media organizations with a strong political angle.
- Social media and content-based platform clients where influence over users is significant.
- Work for a sovereign wealth fund or state-linked enterprise that may involve U.S. government interactions, partnerships with or investments in U.S. companies, or work related to how the foreign entity is perceived in the United States.
- Content moderation topics or services.
- Work involving violent or damaging content, or clients whose business model materially relies on violent or damaging content or pornography.
- Advanced analytics on customer behavior where the CST would access sensitive user data at the individual level.
- Consumer privacy.
- Supply chain resiliency work.
- Marketing or search optimization that could touch at-risk groups, such as teenagers.
- Cost or product work involving HR, productivity, or development databases that could create PII exposure.
- Governance situations where one person controls the company and could override management or the board, especially for new TMT clients or clients not served in the past two years.
- Celebrities or clients with high public visibility.

U.S. engagements involving export-controlled information must operate in the USD IT environment. Outside the United States, teams should seek guidance from the relevant Firm contact.

Engagements that deploy Customer360 are at least medium risk. PRACL may determine that the work is higher risk and requires CSRC review.

Teams should syndicate with TMT Reach and Relevance and Firm Media Relations during risk review discussions where relevant.
