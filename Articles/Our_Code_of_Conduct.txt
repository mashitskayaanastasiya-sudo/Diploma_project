Our Code of Conduct

Our Code of Conduct sets the principles that define how Firm members are expected to act. It builds on the expectation that all Firm members comply with laws, regulations, Firm policies, and Firm standards.

All colleagues are expected to know, understand, commit to, and comply with the Code. The Firm also expects contractors, advisers, suppliers, and others working at the Firm's request to act in a manner consistent with the Code.

The Code is built around seven principles:

- We are grounded in our purpose, mission, and values.
- We build trust through integrity.
- We have a duty to speak up.
- We partner with clients responsibly.
- We are conscious of our impact in our communities.
- We respect one another.
- We are dedicated to doing the right thing.

The Firm's purpose is to create positive, enduring change in the world. Firm members are expected to help clients make distinctive, lasting, and substantial improvements while helping build a Firm that attracts, develops, excites, and retains exceptional people.

Firm members are expected to act with integrity, exercise professional judgment, and seek guidance when the right course of action is unclear. Local laws and regulations may impose stricter requirements, and Firm members must comply with those requirements.

Leaders of people are expected to:

- Role model behavior consistent with the Code, Firm policies and standards, and the law.
- Create an environment where colleagues can raise concerns and different points of view.
- Listen to colleagues who seek advice or raise concerns.
- Take reports of misconduct seriously and escalate appropriately.
- Never tolerate harassment, discrimination, intimidation, or retaliation.

Firm members have a duty to speak up when they see, suspect, or learn about conduct that violates the Code, Firm policies, Firm standards, or the law. Failure to report misconduct may result in discipline.

Reports may be made to appropriate people leadership, HR, professional development, the confidential reporting mechanism, or the channel for partner-related concerns. Reports are reviewed thoroughly and treated confidentially where possible.

The Firm does not tolerate retaliation against anyone who raises a concern in good faith or participates in a review or investigation.

Firm members must select clients and engagements responsibly. This includes following risk policies, completing required assessments, and seeking guidance from risk, legal, and other Firm functions for complex or high-risk matters.

Firm members must protect confidentiality. Confidential information should only be shared with people who have a need to know to serve a client or perform their job duties. Firm members must follow client confidentiality obligations and may not move Firm or client data to personal or unapproved systems.

Firm members may not trade in single-name publicly traded securities. Firm members and covered household members must not make personal investments while in possession of material nonpublic information.

Firm members must safeguard data. Data should be collected, used, stored, transferred, and retained only as needed and through approved tools and controls. Firm members should not request or provide data without a need to know.

Firm technology assets must be used responsibly. Firm members should use Firm-issued technology appropriately, use approved applications for work, and protect devices, accounts, passwords, access cards, and physical workspaces from unauthorized access.

Firm members must avoid actual or perceived conflicts of interest. Conflicts may include institutional, business, financial, staffing, or hiring conflicts. If a situation could influence or appear to influence independence, objectivity, or judgment, Firm members should seek guidance.

The Firm is conscious of its impact in communities. Firm members should support social impact, environmental sustainability, and the responsible and ethical use of artificial intelligence.

Firm members must respect one another. The Firm expects an inclusive, respectful, and caring meritocracy. Harassment, discrimination, intimidation, and retaliation are not tolerated.

Firm members should communicate responsibly, including in business communications, publications, and public-facing materials. Firm members should use appropriate tone, accuracy, professionalism, and approved channels.

Firm members are responsible for workplace health, well-being, and safety. They should complete required training, report hazards or incidents, avoid violence or threats, and not work or participate in Firm business while impaired by drugs or alcohol.

Firm members must never engage in bribery or corruption. The Firm does not offer, promise, accept, request, solicit, or pay bribes in any form or value. Bribes may include cash, gifts, travel, meals, entertainment, employment offers, charitable donations, political contributions, or other benefits.

Firm members must interact properly with government officials. Providing anything of value to government officials carries heightened risk and may require pre-approval. Firm members may not lobby on behalf of clients, negotiate with government officials on behalf of clients, or serve as an independent adviser to satisfy regulatory requirements unless permitted under Firm policy.

Firm members must comply with trade controls and sanctions. They must consider export, import, and sanctions requirements when moving services, information, technology, software, or goods across borders or when engaging clients, suppliers, or other parties.

Firm members must compete fairly. They must not engage in or assist anticompetitive conduct, including price fixing, bid rigging, output restrictions, no-poach agreements, or improper sharing of competitively sensitive information.

Firm members must protect individual privacy. Personal data should be collected only when needed, used fairly and transparently, shared only with those who need to know, protected through approved tools, and disposed of securely when no longer needed.

Firm members must maintain complete and accurate records. Business, financial, accounting, expense, billing, regulatory, and client engagement records must be truthful, timely, accurate, legible, and complete.

When facing uncertain or challenging decisions, Firm members should pause and consider:

- Is the action consistent with the Code, Firm policies and standards, and the law?
- Is the action in the interest of the client, the Firm, or other stakeholders?
- Would others view the action as appropriate?
- Would I be proud of the action if others learned about it?

If the answer is no or uncertain, Firm members should seek guidance before acting.

Violations of the Code, Firm policies, Firm standards, or the law may result in discipline, up to and including separation from the Firm.
