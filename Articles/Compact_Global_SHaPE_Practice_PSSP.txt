Global SHaPE Compact - PSSS Engagements

This compact provides guidance for public, social, and state-owned sector engagements. It does not supersede the Client Service Policy or other Firm policies and guidelines. Partners and client service teams must comply with all applicable Firm policies and relevant sector compacts.

Public sector engagements are subject to an additional country selection process.

Work that is not approved in this space should not be performed.

Some work requires exceptional Client Service Risk Committee approval.

High-risk work requires Client Service Risk Committee approval with input from PRACL and other relevant cell leaders, plus ongoing deliverable review.

Medium-risk-plus work requires Client Service Risk Committee approval with input from PRACL and other relevant cell leaders.

Medium-risk work requires PRACL approval with input from other relevant cell leaders.

Work not approved or requiring exceptional scrutiny includes:

- Work for prisons or corrections.
- Work directly relating to front-line operations of police or law enforcement organizations.
- Border security operations related to enforcement of irregular or unauthorized migration, including decision-making affecting migrants' rights.
- Direct support for combat operations, such as targeting or strategy.
- Work focused on defeating a specific nation.
- Analytics or AI work with potential for bias or targeting individuals or vulnerable populations.
- Work that could cause meaningful harm to client personnel or individuals outside the client organization, including vulnerable populations.
- Work for political campaigns, political parties, or individuals rather than institutions.
- Policy or regulatory advocacy or sensitive recommendations.
- Performance-based fees with uncapped upside.
- Work requiring FARA registration with the United States Department of Justice.
- Defense and security clients in countries below the applicable EIU Democracy Index threshold.
- Work for public, social, or state-owned sector clients in high-risk countries with no Firm office, except international aid and humanitarian support approved exceptionally.
- Defense work involving sensitive foreign military sales.
- Partnering outside Firm client capabilities, such as with legal, engineering, or investment firms, except where allowed.
- Work for institutions with a pattern of corruption, anticompetitive, abusive, or illegal conduct.
- Work where there are reasons to doubt a client leader's personal integrity.
- Work likely to be subject to government inquiries, parliamentary hearings, audits, or other enhanced scrutiny.
- Social media or public sentiment analysis for PSSS clients.
- Work that could be perceived as negatively affecting citizen welfare or the price of products or services for citizens, including vulnerable populations.
- Work for intelligence, police, or justice agencies in countries above the EIU threshold where restrictions still apply to corrections and enforcement divisions.
- All-of-government reform programs in countries under the EIU threshold that involve interactions with defense, intelligence, justice, or police.
- Work related to immigration, border security, surveillance, or other security-related areas with high social sensitivity.
- Engagements with very high contract value relative to the client budget or Firm revenue.
- High-risk procurement vehicles or processes.
- Work that could raise geopolitical sensitivities or divisive public debate.

Restrictions on police, prisons, corrections, and border agencies do not typically include emergency response, front-line rescue, environmental protection, customs and excise, or mental health topics, but those should be reviewed case by case with practice and client service risk.

Medium-risk parameters include:

- Serving industry regulators.
- Fact-base support for regulatory or public-policy decision-making on sensitive topics.
- Work for non-U.S. clients with a government nexus where any aspect relates to the United States, including government interaction, attracting U.S. investment or partners, or influencing U.S. public perception.
- Large cost-cutting efforts, privatizations, or closures affecting more than 1,000 people.
- Independent evaluations or reports on the performance of government entities other than the client agency.
- Contingent fee arrangements.
- Large fees or potential perceived conflicts.
- Consulting intermediaries.
- Pro bono work followed by directly related paid work.
- Work involving subcontractors mandated by policy.
- Engagements supporting military acquisition processes with potential adverse effects on existing clients.

The SHaPE Practice focuses public sector work globally on specified client focus countries. Work in focus countries requires standard approvals based on risk level.

Public sector work in non-focus countries requires exceptional approvals from the relevant office managing partner, regional SHaPE sector leader, Global Public Sector Lead, and Client Service Risk Committee.
