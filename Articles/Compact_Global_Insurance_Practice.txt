Compact Global Insurance Practice

This compact does not supersede other Firm policies or guidelines. Partners and CSTs remain responsible for complying with the Client Service Policy, M&A and Serving Competitors guidance, and any other relevant sector compacts for the specific client or engagement context.

Work that is not approved includes:

- Actuarial certification.
- Pricing work that could be perceived as anti-competitive.
- Regulatory advocacy, public policy advocacy, or lobbying.
- Indemnity for specific claims.
- Investment-related advice.
- Insurance work in areas where the Firm otherwise does not work, including cannabis, tobacco, opioids, civilian firearms, or corrections facilities.

High-risk work requires CSRC approval with input from PRACL and other relevant cell leaders, as well as ongoing deliverable review. Examples include:

- Indemnity accuracy improvement on bodily injury or material damage claims.
- Advanced analytics or GenAI-based claims transformation.
- Claims fraud management.
- Work in response to a specific government inquiry.
- Capital and balance sheet management where the work relates to financial resource management, risk appetite, or specific investment regulations.
- Work for industry associations.
- Work for government-owned or government-backed insurers and reinsurers.
- Work for specialty insurers covering risks associated with potentially controversial technology.
- Fees at risk based on milestones or deliverables more within the Firm's control, including operational metrics or referral rates.
- Fees at risk based on milestones or deliverables outside the Firm's direct control, such as volume, where CSRC review may be required.

Medium-risk work requires PRACL approval with input from other relevant cell leaders. Examples include:

- Work related to market-based pricing.
- Product development.
- Sales campaigns.
- Investment management.
- Stress testing.
- Other claims topics.
- Regulatory work that helps a client comply with new regulation with established requirements.
- Work that could disproportionately affect smaller or vulnerable populations, including work supporting withdrawal from or moratoriums on higher-risk areas where insurance availability or affordability could be affected.
- DnA projects involving policyholder data, including analytics that segment policyholders by potentially sensitive characteristics.
- Client-facing Firm software.
- GenAI work.

CoRE registration is required for relevant DnA, client-facing software, and GenAI work. Teams should contact the regional Practice Manager and relevant risk leaders for guidance before proceeding.
