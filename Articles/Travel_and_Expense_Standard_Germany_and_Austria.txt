Travel and Expense Standard - Germany and Austria

This standard supplements the Travel and Expense Policy and applies to Firm members assigned to Germany or Austria. It provides operational rules for business travel, expense submission, transportation, lodging, meals, events, gifts, and other reimbursable expenses.

A business trip occurs when a Firm member performs professional activity outside their permanent residence and regular place of work. Commuting between a residence and assigned Firm office is not a business trip and is not reimbursable.

A business trip may begin or end at the Firm member's permanent residence or assigned Firm office in the same country as the assigned office. It may not begin or end at a secondary residence, from abroad, or at an airport outside the assigned office country.

Firm members should use the approved online booking tool or contact the home office travel team.

Only actual incurred expenses are reimbursable unless this standard expressly allows otherwise. Prepaid value cards, estimated expenses, and per diem expenses are not reimbursable except where stated.

Expense requirements include:

- Submit expenses no later than 60 days after incurring them.
- Provide full detailed receipts unless the standard says otherwise.
- Provide receipts for all cash payments, including who was paid and the city and country of payment.
- Submit full hotel bills addressed to the Firm office or assigned Firm legal entity.
- Use a corporate card when available; if unavailable or not accepted, use a personal card, debit card, or cash.

Firm members should make sustainable travel choices when travel is required.

Air and rail travel rules include:

- Use the lowest eligible fare within the required departure-time window.
- Consider rail as an alternative to flights.
- Short-haul flights on certain routes are not permitted unless part of a long-haul connection.
- Seat selection fees within the same cabin are reimbursable.
- Premium seat upgrades are not reimbursable unless obtained for free through eligible status.
- For non-client travel, business class is allowed for flights of four hours or more; economy is required for flights under four hours.
- Business-class thresholds are based on total flight time, excluding layovers.
- First or second class rail travel is permitted.

Accommodation rules include:

- Trips may start or end only from the primary residence or assigned Firm office.
- Accommodation in the home city is generally not reimbursable.
- Preferred hotels must be used when available.
- Standard room type should be selected.
- Accommodation-sharing services such as Airbnb are not allowed.
- If no suitable preferred hotel is available, the travel service should book within the city maximum rate.
- Extended stays or corporate apartments may be used when appropriate and cost effective.
- Hotel invoices should be addressed to the assigned Firm legal entity and office, not to the traveler personally.
- No-show charges and cancellation fees are not reimbursable unless exceptional circumstances apply.

The following hotel services are not reimbursable:

- Laundry.
- Mini bar.
- Room service.
- Gyms outside the hotel.
- Spa treatments.

Ground transportation rules include:

- Commuting between home and assigned office is a personal expense and not reimbursable, except limited late-night exceptions may apply.
- E-scooter expenses are not reimbursable.
- Preferred taxi or ride-hail services should be used where available.
- Taxi and ride-hail expenses are reimbursable up to EUR 100 per ride.
- Black car services, limousines, private chauffeurs, and premium ride-hail services are not reimbursable.
- A Firm car is the preferred ground transportation mode for Firm members issued with one.
- Rental cars should use preferred vendors and are generally permitted only on business workdays.
- Weekend rental cars are not permitted.
- Additional rental car insurance should not be purchased when insurance is included through Firm Travel.
- Personal car use is reimbursable only when the colleague does not have a Firm car.
- Parking at train stations, airports, or client sites is reimbursable when reasonable.
- Parking at the assigned Firm location is a personal expense.
- Bridge, highway, and other tolls are reimbursable when incurred for business travel.

Meal rules for German employees include daily allowances for personal meals while on business trips. Allowances vary based on trip length, destination, and government-published rates. Allowances are reduced when breakfast, lunch, or dinner is provided free of charge.

Meal rules for Austrian employees include daily allowances based on time away from the assigned office or permanent residence. Rates vary by destination and time spent in each location.

In-office meals are not reimbursable. Team dinners may be reimbursable as occasional client service team dinners or through a representation budget when requirements are met.

Client service team dinners may be reimbursed up to EUR 100 per person, up to two times per month, charged to the client code, and planned in close alignment with the responsible partner.

Client service team events may include a dinner and an additional professional or social activity. A total spend limit of EUR 200 is suggested, including the dinner. Events may need to be registered for tax treatment.

Client events must comply with the Anti-Corruption Policy. Required approvals must be obtained before incurring expenses on behalf of non-Firm members. Itemized invoices are required.

Team room refreshments at a client location may be expensed when they are reasonable snacks and refreshments purchased locally in moderate quantities. Items such as smoothies, energy drinks, sandwiches, pastries, nutrition supplements, and food-like meals are personal expenses.

Weekend travel rules include:

- Travel back to the home location is generally weekly when staffed in Europe and every three weeks as a guideline when staffed outside Europe.
- Staying in the work city for a weekend requires prior travel team approval.
- Spouse or significant-other travel may be allowed for longer assignments outside Europe only with required approvals and conditions.
- Leveraged fly-out travel to an alternative non-home city is not allowed for tax reasons.

Events requiring overnight stay and exceeding the applicable threshold must be registered through the Firm event process.

Internal gifts arranged by the office may be reimbursable for specific occasions. Gifts to colleagues not sponsored by the office are not reimbursable.

External gifts must comply with the Anti-Corruption Policy. Additional rules apply to government officials and public-sector clients or potential clients.

Other expense rules include:

- Personally sourced phone or internet services are not reimbursable.
- In-flight Wi-Fi needed for work is reimbursable as needed.
- IT equipment and teamroom technology must be ordered through the Global Help Desk.
- Professional publications and market research require prior approval from the relevant knowledge or research team.
- Professional membership fees are generally not reimbursable unless required for the Firm role.
- Visa fees for business travel are reimbursable.
- Passport renewal due to ordinary expiration is personal; renewal due to insufficient pages from extensive Firm travel may be reimbursable.
- Shipping engagement-related materials between client site and office is reimbursable.
- Corporate or credit card late fees are not reimbursable.

Exceptions are expected to be rare. Exception requests must be raised with the travel team before the expense is incurred. After-the-fact exceptions cannot be approved for compliance reasons.

Travel to restricted jurisdictions requires case-by-case approval. Firm members carrying items beyond personal luggage between countries should seek guidance in advance, especially for samples, prototypes, lab equipment, blueprints, high-technology or defense items, or export-controlled information.
