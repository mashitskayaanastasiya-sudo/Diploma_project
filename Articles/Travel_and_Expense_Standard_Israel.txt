Travel and Expense Standard - Israel

This standard supplements the Travel and Expense Policy and applies to Firm members assigned to Israel.

Only actual incurred expenses are reimbursable unless this standard states otherwise. Prepaid value cards, estimated expenses, and per diem expenses are not reimbursable except where expressly permitted.

Expense requirements include:

- Submit all expenses through Rydoo within 60 days of incurring them.
- Submit full hotel bills with hotel expense claims.
- Address hotel bills to the Firm office address.
- Provide full detailed receipts unless the standard says otherwise.
- Provide receipts for all cash payments, including who was paid and the city and country of payment.
- Use a corporate card where accepted.
- Use a personal form of payment or cash only when permitted.

Firm members should make sustainable choices when travel is required.

Air and rail travel rules include:

- For client-related air travel, business class is permitted for flights of four hours or more; economy class is required for flights under four hours.
- Exceptions require prior managerial approval.
- For non-client travel, economy class is the default.
- Non-client travel should be booked at least two weeks in advance.
- Business-class thresholds are based on total flight time, excluding layovers.
- If a non-stop flight is available, the cabin allowed on the non-stop flight determines eligibility for connecting routes.
- For round trips where one direction meets the business-class threshold and the other does not, business class is allowed in both directions.
- For rail travel, business or first class is permitted for trips of one hour or more.

Accommodation in the home country or home city is not permitted except in exceptional circumstances with written approval from the appropriate leader.

If no preferred hotel is available or suitable, the travel service should offer a hotel at or below the destination's maximum allowed rate.

Extended stays or corporate apartments may be offered by the travel service in exceptional cases with approval from the appropriate leader.

Hotel invoices should list the Firm home office address, not the traveler's personal address.

No-show charges are not reimbursable except in exceptional circumstances. If accommodation is not needed, the traveler should cancel the reservation through the travel service or booking tool where available.

Hotel service reimbursement rules include:

- Laundry for business-related items is allowed for stays of three nights or more.
- Mini-bar snacks and soft drinks are allowed within meal limits.
- Alcohol from the mini-bar is not reimbursable.
- Hotel telephone, movies, gyms outside the hotel, and spa treatments are not reimbursable.

Ground transportation rules include:

- Transportation between home and home office is reimbursable only outside business hours, before 8:00 a.m., after 8:30 p.m., or on public holidays, and requires managerial approval.
- Transportation between home and client is reimbursable.
- Transportation between office and client is reimbursable.
- Centrally billed taxi services should be used where available.
- Rental cars are permitted only with approval and for study purposes.
- Additional rental car insurance should generally be declined if standard coverage is included.
- Personal or private use of a rental car is prohibited.
- Fines, speeding tickets, car washes, and specialty or luxury vehicle upgrades are not reimbursable.
- Parking at a client site and tolls for client travel are reimbursable.

Meal rules include:

- Meals abroad are reimbursable within stated breakfast, lunch, and dinner caps.
- Breakfast abroad should be included in the hotel room cost where possible.
- Breakfast is not reimbursable when working in Israel.
- Food and drink purchased at the airport are reimbursable.
- Client team room snacks are reimbursable with managerial approval.
- Individual or group meals in the home city may be handled through the local monthly meal benefit.
- Team events require a guest list and managerial approval.
- Client-attended events must comply with the Anti-Corruption Policy and applicable meal limits.

Weekend travel rules include:

- Travel back to the home location is generally weekly when staffed in Europe and every two to three weeks when staffed outside Europe.
- Staying in the work city for a weekend is allowed; corporate hotel and meal expenses are reimbursable within normal limits.
- Spouse or significant-other travel may be reimbursable under the stated conditions, limited to restricted economy airfare booked by the Firm travel service.
- No other spouse or significant-other costs are reimbursable.
- Travel to an alternative non-home city instead of traveling home is not allowed and is treated as a personal cost.

Events over the applicable threshold that require overnight stay must be registered through the Firm event process.

Internal gifts may be allowed for appreciation, sickness, bereavement, and milestone events when approval and value requirements are met. Gift cards should not be given and are not reimbursable.

External gifts to clients must comply with the Anti-Corruption Policy. Additional rules apply to government officials and public-sector clients or potential clients.

Other expense rules include:

- Personally sourced telecommunications devices or service are not reimbursable.
- Home internet or Wi-Fi is reimbursable up to the local monthly cap.
- IT supplies should use local office procurement processes; last-minute client-site purchases require explanation and approval.
- Certain digital subscriptions to well-known financial or business publications are reimbursable up to the local monthly cap.
- Other publications require practice knowledge team approval.
- Professional membership fees are generally not reimbursable unless necessary for the Firm role.
- Market research requires practice knowledge team approval or a research request.
- Office supplies should be obtained from office services when possible.
- Approved conference and networking expenses may be reimbursable.
- Visa fees for business travel are reimbursable.
- Ordinary passport renewal is personal; renewal due to insufficient pages from extensive Firm travel may be reimbursable.
- Engagement-related delivery and postage are reimbursable.
- Corporate or credit card late fees are not reimbursable.
- Work-related holiday cancellation costs may be reimbursable within the stated limits.
- In-flight Wi-Fi is reimbursable.
- Charity donations are not reimbursable.

Exceptions are expected to be rare and require written approval from a partner or FSP leader.

Travel to restricted jurisdictions requires case-by-case approval. Firm members carrying items beyond personal luggage between countries should seek guidance in advance, especially for samples, prototypes, lab equipment, blueprints, high-technology or defense items, or export-controlled information.
