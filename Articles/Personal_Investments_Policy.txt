Personal Investments Policy

Firm members may not make any personal investment in a public company or private entity while in possession of material non-public information (MNPI) concerning that investment.

This policy governs investments undertaken by Firm members in their individual capacity, including publicly traded securities, private entity investments, currencies, cryptocurrencies, commodities, and other financial instruments.

Purchases and sales of single name publicly traded securities are not permitted by any Firm member or their covered Household Members.

Investing in private entities must not create the appearance of a conflict of interest and must be consistent with this policy.

This policy applies to all Firm members. Firm members must also comply with applicable laws.

For publicly traded securities, the policy also applies to covered Household Members, including spouses, domestic partners, and dependents who reside in the Firm member's home for the majority of the year. Firm members are expected to inform covered Household Members about the policy and require them to comply.

This policy applies to:

- The purchase or sale of equity or debt securities, options, commodities, currencies, cryptocurrencies, or other financial instruments.
- The exercise of options or derivatives.
- Any account in which a Firm member or Household Member is a direct or indirect owner or may exercise control.

Firm members may not:

- Buy or sell single name publicly traded equity or debt securities or other financial instruments unless expressly permitted.
- Use the Firm's name or resources for investment purposes.
- Make investments that conflict with Firm or client responsibilities.
- Engage in investment activities that negatively affect their commitment to the Firm or its clients.
- Use Firm or client confidential information for investing activities.
- Use MNPI or confidential client or Firm information for investment purposes.
- Disclose MNPI or confidential client or Firm information to others who do not need it for client service, including Household Members.
- Recommend the purchase or sale of a security to another person while in possession of MNPI about the security or issuer.

Firm members are responsible for promptly raising any personal situation that may create an actual or perceived conflict of interest. Examples include a household member who works for a client or competitor, a personal investment in a client or its target, or approved outside activities that may affect client, colleague, or Firm obligations.

Investments in private entities must meet these requirements:

- The investment must be passive, meaning an investment of money only, with no informal advising or other time commitment.
- The opportunity must not have been learned of or presented through a client or Firm situation.
- Firm members may not serve a private entity client while holding an investment in that client.
- For private equity funds, Firm members may not serve the fund or an entity owned by that fund.
- Firm members may serve a client that is a fund or portfolio company of a fund in which they are invested only if the investment is held within a pooled, multi-manager fund such as a fund of funds or secondary fund.

Investments in private equity and hedge funds must also meet these requirements:

- The Firm member must have no input or ability to control the investment mix.
- The investment must be in the fund, and the fund must determine how the money is invested.
- The Firm member must not know that any client they serve is part of the investment mix at the time of investment.
- If the Firm member later learns that the fund has invested in a client they serve, they must promptly report the matter to the PIP Compliance team for review.

Holding single name publicly traded securities may be permitted if they were acquired before joining COMPANY, before August 1, 2022 in compliance with the Personal Investments Policy, through inheritance, or otherwise in compliance with the policy.

Permitted trades and accounts include:

- Mutual funds, index funds, ETFs other than single-stock ETFs, money market funds, currencies, commodities, cryptocurrencies not deemed securities by the SEC, and U.S. Section 529 plans.
- National government securities and agency debt securities issued by government-sponsored enterprises.
- Accounts managed by an unrelated professional advisor or broker with full discretionary authority and written instructions not to discuss trading decisions with the Firm member or Household Member.
- Trades executed for an unrelated third party by a Household Member who is an employee of a broker-dealer or investment adviser and subject to their employer's insider trading policy.
- Trades by a Household Member in their employer's securities or options under the employer's policies and procedures.
- Irrevocable pre-established trading plans or orders put in place before May 9, 2022 or before joining the Firm.

Prohibited trades include:

- Purchase and sale of single name publicly traded equity and debt securities.
- Exercise of single name options and derivatives.
- Purchase and sale of state or local municipal securities.

Exceptions to sell single name publicly traded securities may be available in limited circumstances, usually for financial need or timing not of the Firm member's choosing. Requests must be submitted through PIPS and approved in writing by the PIP Compliance team before any trade occurs.

New Firm members have a 30-day grace period after joining the Firm during which they may sell publicly traded securities owned upon joining, provided they are not in possession of MNPI.

The Firm may need to maintain lists of Firm members who have access to inside information under EU Market Abuse Regulation. Unauthorized use or disclosure of inside information is prohibited.

Firm members must comply with this policy, act with integrity, and report violations. Local law controls where it imposes stricter requirements.

The Firm does not tolerate retaliation for reporting in good faith or participating in a review or investigation.

Failure to comply may result in disciplinary action, including separation from the Firm. The Firm may request trading and investment records from Firm members and Household Members.
