Outside Activities Policy

This policy applies to partners and Firm members, subject to applicable law.

Firm members may not engage in outside activities that create a real or perceived conflict of interest, could adversely affect the Firm or its clients, or could create confusion about when the individual is acting as a Firm member.

New hires must disclose any ongoing outside activity after accepting an offer to join the Firm so that HR can assess conflicts before the start date.

Firm members may not sit on the board of directors of any for-profit company, whether privately held, publicly listed, or state owned, unless a specific exception applies under the Standard on Board Memberships.

Firm members seeking formal board service with a social sector, public sector, or healthcare organization must obtain express permission from the SHaPE Practice before pursuing the role. Permission depends on whether the organization's mission is consistent with Firm values, whether it is or may become a paying client, and whether the role could create client, reputational, or other Firm risks.

Firm members may not:

- Be employed by or under contract to provide goods or services to another organization while they are Firm members.
- Provide consulting or similar professional services to a third party for remuneration outside normal Firm client service.
- Act as an expert for other entities, including through expert network services.
- Build, operate, or actively manage a for-profit business.
- Raise financing for a for-profit business.
- Provide advice, services, or support to help candidates apply to professional services firms, except occasional advice to family or friends or normal Firm recruiting activity.
- Use the Firm's name or resources in outside activities.
- Use their position at the Firm or Firm relationships for the benefit of outside activities.
- Run for political office or accept an official role in a political campaign or public official's office.
- Undertake political activity that could appear to be on behalf of the Firm, suggest Firm support for a political position, candidate, or party, or conflict with government client interests.
- Maintain online or social media activities that create conflict or reputational risk, including remunerated activities through advertising, sponsorship, marketing, subscriptions, gifts, products, or in-kind benefits.

Some outside activities are permitted if they do not interfere with commitments to the Firm or clients, do not involve representing the organization to third parties, and do not use the Firm's name or resources.

Permitted activities may include:

- Volunteer work for non-profit or recreational organizations that are not Firm clients and are not controversial.
- Behind-the-scenes creation or operation of a non-profit organization that does not create reputational harm, interfere with client relationships, or involve consulting-like services.
- Short-term teaching positions.
- Activities needed to maintain professional licenses, such as pro bono legal work or occasional clinical practice where applicable.
- Pro bono legal services by Firm legal colleagues under the relevant guidance.
- Informal and occasional advice to friends or family businesses that does not involve an active operational role.
- Passive minority ownership of 49% or less of a private sector business, subject to conflict disclosure and potential recusal.
- Secondments or non-profit fellowships for non-partner colleagues after appropriate approvals.
- In Australia, acting as director of a self-managed superannuation fund.

If a private entity in which a Firm member holds passive ownership becomes publicly traded, the Personal Investments Policy applies.

Board membership restrictions include:

- No Firm member may serve on a for-profit board of directors unless an exception applies.
- Only partners may serve on private-sector advisory boards for for-profit companies.
- Non-profit, public sector, social sector, and healthcare board or advisory board roles require express SHaPE Practice approval.
- Restrictions apply to new hires with pre-existing board roles and to external workers who spend more than half their time working with the Firm.

Outside activities not expressly permitted by the policy require disclosure to local HR and written approval from local HR or a Firm leader appointed by HR before the activity begins.

Secondments and nonprofit fellowships require review and approval through the relevant Firm process.

Non-partner exception requests, including requests involving occasional remuneration in money or in kind, must be reported to and approved by the HR Manager after a risk assessment. Appeals may be escalated to the Regional HR Leader.

Partners seeking exceptions should raise requests to Partner Personnel.

Firm members must comply with this policy, act with integrity, and report violations. If local law is stricter, Firm members must follow local law.

The Firm does not tolerate retaliation for good-faith reporting or participation in a review or investigation.

Failure to comply may result in disciplinary action, including separation from the Firm. For third parties engaged by the Firm, violations may result in consequences up to and including termination of the arrangement.
