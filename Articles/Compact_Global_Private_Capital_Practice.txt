Compact Global Private Capital Practice

This compact does not supersede other Firm policies or guidelines. Partners and CSTs remain responsible for complying with the Client Service Policy, M&A and Serving Competitors guidance, and any other relevant sector compact for the specific client or engagement context.

Work that is not approved includes:

- Work for clients relating to trading public securities that does not meet the strict criteria for approved exceptions under the IRS Standard, including outside-in diligence support or work for a client under significant trading restrictions.
- Work involving activities that would require the Firm to register as a broker-dealer, such as investment advice, facilitating introductions for a transaction outside the strict criteria in the M&A Standard, soliciting investment, or receiving transaction-based compensation or satisfaction fees.
- Work for clients engaged in activist investment efforts, such as hostile public efforts to affect a public company's strategy, management, or ownership.
- Work for clients outside the Firm's service line boundaries for the client or target industry, such as opioids or for-profit prisons.
- Work for SPACs that are not registered with the SEC.

High-risk work requires CSRC approval with input from PRACL and other relevant cell leaders, as well as ongoing deliverable review. Examples include:

- Support for SEC-registered SPACs or similar vehicles.
- Staffing or involving target CST members on an engagement for a client considering a deal with a public target.
- Work where any portion of the Firm's fee will be paid in equity, synthetic equity, or a value-based arrangement, including capital-enabled consulting or equity in lieu of cash fees, whether in a PE client fund or a portfolio company.
- Work for clients with non-U.S. government ties, such as sovereign wealth funds, where any aspect of the work relates to the United States. This includes introductions to U.S.-based companies or people, or providing advice or materials to influence U.S.-based companies or people.

Medium-risk plus work requires CSRC approval with input from PRACL and other relevant cell leaders. Examples include:

- Work for clients in connection with potential investment in public securities where the client is under significant trading restrictions with the target.
- Outside-in diligence support connected to a potential client investment in public securities.
- Diligence or transaction-related support where the Firm has an institutional conflict, such as a Firm equity holding or service line that competes with the target.
- Work for a public company board member on any study relating to that public company.

Medium-risk work requires PRACL approval with input from other relevant cell leaders. Examples include:

- Staffing or involving target CST members on an engagement for a PE client considering a deal with a private target.
- Work for clients or targets where the work would be medium risk under any relevant sector compact or risk guidance, including DnA guidance.
- Any performance-based fee arrangement at the PE fund level, as opposed to the portfolio company level.

Teams should confirm the risk level early, review the relevant sector compact for the client and target, and escalate potential conflicts before staffing or beginning work.
