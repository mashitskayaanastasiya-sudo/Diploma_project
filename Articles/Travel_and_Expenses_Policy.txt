Travel and Expenses Policy

This policy sets a framework for reimbursable business expenses. If the policy does not say that an expense can be reimbursed, the expense should be treated as non-reimbursable.

Engagement-related expenses must be charged to the code they are directly and incrementally attributable to.

All Firm-related travel must be booked through Firm Travel using a Firm-approved online booking tool or the travel service for the Firm member's assigned COMPANY office. Firm members must also ensure they have any visas required for travel.

Travelers may not travel to, or commit to work leading to travel to, destinations rated High Risk or Extreme Risk except in limited approved circumstances. Firm Security can advise whether a destination is High Risk or Extreme Risk.

Current regulations and COMPANY policy prohibit working in, taking Firm devices to, or using Firm resources in Restricted Jurisdictions. Personal travel to Restricted Jurisdictions is also subject to restrictions.

This policy applies to all Firm members and External Workers. It also applies where the Firm pays travel and related expenses for non-Firm members such as significant others, spouses, family members, alumni, candidates, offerees, and clients.

All authorized travel and expenses, including those for clients and non-Firm members, must comply with the Firm's Anti-Corruption Policy.

If this policy or the supporting Travel and Expense Standard for the assigned COMPANY office does not state that an expense is reimbursable, the expense is non-reimbursable unless written approval is received from the Office Managing Partner before the expense is incurred.

Firm members are expected to use Firm procurement processes for products and services rather than buying them directly and seeking reimbursement, except in rare and urgent circumstances.

If a Firm member overclaims expenses that are not eligible for reimbursement, COMPANY may deduct the overclaimed amounts from amounts owed to the Firm member unless prohibited by applicable law. If deduction is not permitted, the Firm member must repay the Firm.

Firm members who are uncertain whether an expense is consistent with the policy should consult Finance in advance.

Air and rail travel requirements:

- Firm-related air and rail travel must be booked through Firm Travel.
- Firm members must select a Firm Preferred Fare.
- The default booking class is Economy, but Business may be booked where the applicable Travel and Expense Standard permits it.
- First class long-haul intercontinental travel is allowed only in exceptional cases with prior written approval from the Office Managing Partner.
- Pregnant Firm members may use business class or equivalent on any charge code and itinerary duration after HR adds them to the exception list.
- Firm members with disabilities may request reasonable accommodation through HR if business class is medically necessary.
- Seat selection fees within the same cabin and checked bags are reimbursable.
- Personal modifications to travel plans, upgrades, and voluntary downgrades for personal gain are not reimbursable.
- Airport fast-track security or passport control programs are reimbursable if they do not include additional features such as lounge access, parking, or meet-and-greet services.
- Airline club memberships are not reimbursable. Occasional lounge day passes may be reimbursable when necessary for Firm business.
- Visa fees for Firm business travel are reimbursable.
- Passport renewal due to an expired passport is personal. Passport renewal due to insufficient pages from Firm travel is reimbursable.

Charter flights may be used only in rare circumstances when travel is critical and no viable commercial flight options exist. Charter requests require written pre-approval from the Office Managing Partner and must be arranged through preferred charter brokers or operators and Firm travel service.

Firm members who choose to live in a city different from their assigned COMPANY office are personally responsible for travel between their residence and assigned office, and for related accommodation and meal expenses, unless a local rule provides otherwise.

Accommodation requirements:

- Hotel costs are reimbursable when traveling outside the assigned COMPANY office city on Firm business.
- Firm members must use preferred hotels where available and book through Firm Travel.
- Peer-to-peer property rentals such as Airbnb or Vrbo are not allowed.
- Upgrades are not reimbursable unless part of the Firm's standard arrangements.
- Hotel invoices should list the assigned COMPANY office address, not the Firm member's personal home address.
- Hotel gym fees may be reimbursable if charged by the hotel.
- Firm members may not negotiate rates, amenities, or loyalty rewards directly with hotels.
- Extended stays or corporate apartments of more than one month require applicable approval if not part of approved mobility.

Ground transportation requirements:

- Firm members must use preferred suppliers where available.
- Reasonable and customary gratuities are reimbursable and should be paid with the corporate card when possible.
- Rental cars must use preferred rental suppliers where available and be booked through Firm Travel.

Meal requirements:

- Individual and group meals outside the assigned COMPANY office city are reimbursable subject to local limits.
- Meals in the assigned COMPANY office city when working outside normal business hours are reimbursable only where local rules permit and the Firm member is working from the office.
- Meals while working from home outside normal business hours are not reimbursable.
- Occasional team celebratory meals or events may be reimbursable subject to local limits.
- If one Firm member pays for more than one person, they must provide attendee names with the expense claim.
- Meal gratuities must be within daily meal limits.

Payment method requirements:

- Eligible Firm members must acquire and use a corporate card for business expenses where available and accepted.
- Firm members are responsible for managing and paying their corporate card accounts.
- The corporate card must not be used for personal expenses.

Expenses paid for non-Firm members in connection with meetings and events must comply with the Anti-Corruption Policy.

Weekend travel options vary by office. Tax implications should be directed to local Finance. Engagement Loan weekend travel follows the assigned COMPANY office policy.

Spouse or significant other travel may create tax implications and may be non-reimbursable by law in some countries.

Gifts to Firm colleagues should generally be arranged through the office. Gifts to colleagues outside that process are not reimbursable. Gifts to clients must comply with the Anti-Corruption Policy. Cash and cash equivalents are not permitted as gifts except for very limited exceptions in the Anti-Corruption Policy.

Expenses must be submitted using Rydoo no later than 60 days after the expense is incurred, with appropriate documentation. Delegating expense report preparation does not remove the Firm member's responsibility for accuracy.

Regional Leaders are responsible for office-specific rules and thresholds that are no less restrictive than this policy.

Exceptions are expected to be rare and require written approval from a Partner or FSP Leader and any additional approvals required by local Travel and Expense Standards.

Travel safety requirements:

- Travel decisions must be free of coercion or inappropriate pressure.
- Firm members must ensure they have the correct passport, visas, work permits, or other required permissions.
- High Risk and Extreme Risk destinations require specific approvals.
- The ED of record or responsible person must ensure travel risks are appropriately mitigated and flag concerning travel in Engage.
- Security and safety concerns must be referred to Firm Security.
- Firm Security retains documentation and approvals for high-risk travel, aviation safety exceptions, and accommodation-sharing exceptions.

The Firm may restrict bookings on a single aircraft to protect continuity of client service and Firm leadership.

Travel to Restricted Jurisdictions has no exceptions for Firm work. Firm members traveling personally to a Restricted Jurisdiction must not use Firm resources to book travel, seek reimbursement, take Firm devices, access Firm systems, or perform Firm work from those locations.

Items carried across borders beyond personal luggage may require government authorization, customs duties, or other fees.

Firm members must comply with this policy, act with integrity, and report violations. Local law controls where it imposes stricter requirements.

The Firm does not tolerate retaliation for reporting in good faith or participating in a review or investigation.

Failure to comply may result in disciplinary action, including separation from the Firm.
