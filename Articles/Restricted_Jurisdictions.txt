Restricted Jurisdictions Under the Firm's International Trade Controls Policy

Services are prohibited in:

- Cuba.
- Iran.
- North Korea.
- Russia.
- Crimea.
- The territories of Donetsk, Kherson, Luhansk, and Zaporizhzhia regions that are not controlled by the government of Ukraine.

No work, Firm travel, or COMPANY services may relate to these jurisdictions.

Firm devices, including phones, computers, SIM cards, and work materials, may not enter these jurisdictions. Phones are allowed in Russia.

Connectivity is blocked for internal and external users located in these jurisdictions through Geo IP blocking.

Services are broadly restricted in:

- Belarus.
- Syria.
- Venezuela.

Sanctions and export controls restrict the Firm from virtually any work related to Belarus and Venezuela. Some private sector projects may be legally permitted, subject to CSRC approval.

Syria remains designated by the United States as a state sponsor of terrorism. Services that directly benefit the Syrian government are restricted.

Certain individuals or companies can be sanctioned because they are designated on sanctions lists or owned by designated persons.

Business with certain counterparties and industries can be restricted, including semiconductors, dual-use industries, Entity List parties, and Military End-Use or Military End-User contexts.

Submit a sanctions screening request through the Firm's sanctions screening portal to check for sanctions risks.
