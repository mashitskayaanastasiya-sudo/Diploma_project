Travel and Expense Standard - Spain and Portugal

This standard supplements the Travel and Expense Policy and applies to Firm members and external workers assigned to Spain and Portugal.

Professional expenses must be submitted through Rydoo. Expenses must be submitted promptly and no later than 60 days after they are incurred. Prior calendar-year expenses must be submitted by January 31 of the following year.

Expense submission rules include:

- Submit original receipts and invoices for all claims.
- Receipts and invoices must be issued in the Firm's name, not the traveler's personal name.
- Claims must reflect exact amounts, including VAT or equivalent local taxes.
- Claims should be submitted in the currency in which the expense was incurred.
- Cash receipts must show who was paid and the city and country of payment.
- Each expense should be uploaded separately.
- No expense is paid without a scanned receipt in Rydoo.
- Additional documentation, such as boarding passes, may be required.

Firm members must use central billing where available. If central billing is unavailable, a corporate card is mandatory where the colleague is eligible. Personal payment may be used only when permitted.

Firm members should choose sustainable travel options, including trains over flights where practical.

Travel arrangements must be made through the travel department or approved booking tool. Travel bookings and amendments must include an authorized charge code.

Air and rail travel rules include:

- Lowest logical fares should be booked within the allowed fare and departure-time thresholds.
- Client travel should generally be booked at least one week in advance, and two weeks for intercontinental routes.
- Leveraged fly-out travel to or from non-home cities requires written approval and must not exceed the cost of traveling home.
- Client-related air travel uses economy class for flights under four hours and business class for flights of four hours or more.
- Non-client travel must use economy class unless office leadership approves an exception.
- Business-class thresholds are based on total flight time, excluding layovers.
- Rail should be used whenever available, especially high-speed routes.
- Rail business, comfort, or premium class may be booked if cheaper than economy air travel for the same itinerary.
- Round-trip tickets using the same carrier and restricted fares are preferred where practical.
- Upgrades may be made at personal expense or with personal miles if the base ticket cost does not increase.
- Downgrading travel class for personal gain is prohibited.
- Seat selection within the same cabin may be reimbursable.
- Personal itinerary changes are personal expenses.
- Boarding passes may be required when airfare is paid directly.

Accommodation rules include:

- Preferred hotels with negotiated Firm rates must be used where available.
- Hotels must be booked through the travel implant or approved booking tool.
- Non-preferred hotels require prior approval as an exception.
- If no suitable preferred hotel is available, the travel implant should book within the destination's maximum allowed rate.
- No-show charges are not reimbursable except in exceptional circumstances.
- Accommodation in the home city is not permitted.
- Accommodation in the home country may be allowed only when sufficiently far from the home office location.
- Extended stays or corporate apartments must use approved channels and require appropriate approval.
- Hotel invoices must list the Firm office address, not the traveler's personal address.

Hotel service reimbursement rules include:

- Laundry is reimbursable for stays of three nights or more for business attire only, within daily and weekly limits.
- Pay TV, movies, gyms outside the hotel, spa treatments, and entertainment while away from home are not reimbursable.
- Mini-bar soft drinks and snacks may be reimbursable within daily allowance.
- Alcoholic beverages are reimbursable only when taken with a meal.

Ground transportation rules include:

- Transportation between home and home office is reimbursable only for genuine business needs outside business hours or on public holidays.
- Transportation to and from client sites is reimbursable.
- Transportation to offsite training or learning events is reimbursable with budget owner approval.
- Wait time and no-show charges are not reimbursable.
- Preferred suppliers with central billing or credit agreements should be used.
- Rental cars are not preferred and may be used only where no better alternative exists.
- Rental cars must be arranged through the travel department.
- Midsize rental cars are the standard; specialty and luxury upgrades are not reimbursable.
- Personal car mileage is reimbursable only for legitimate business travel and within statutory rates.
- Fines remain the personal responsibility of the traveler.
- Parking at client sites is reimbursable.
- Parking at the assigned Firm location, bridge tolls, highway tolls, and similar tolls are not reimbursable.

Meal rules include:

- Business-trip meals and out-of-town client-site meals are reimbursable within breakfast, lunch, and dinner limits.
- Room-service meal limits may be higher within the stated caps.
- Receipts must match the reimbursement amount requested.
- Supermarket and grocery expenses are not reimbursable.
- Team room snacks may be reimbursed through approved central billing where available.
- Work-from-home meals are not reimbursable.
- Lunch at a client site in the home city may be reimbursable within limits.
- Late office or client-site dinners may be reimbursable after the stated time.
- Weekend meals without explanation are denied.
- Team events require a meaningful purpose, thoughtful agenda, appropriate location, and local finance or leadership pre-approval.
- Occasional team celebrations may be reimbursable within the per-person limit and should include the engagement director or partner and most of the team.
- Client meals must comply with the Anti-Corruption Policy and may require advance approval.
- Public-sector meals and gifts have lower thresholds and stricter approval requirements.
- Alcohol reimbursement should be reasonable and only with a meal.
- Meal claims with colleagues or clients must include a full attendee list.
- Reasonable tips are reimbursable when paid by corporate card.

Weekend travel rules include:

- Travel home is generally allowed on weekends within Europe and every two weeks outside Europe.
- Staying at an offsite location for the weekend makes transportation, meals, and other weekend expenses personal; the Firm covers only comparable working-week hotel cost.
- Spouse or significant-other travel may be reimbursed instead of the Firm member returning home, within approval, booking, cost, and tax requirements.

Events requiring overnight stay and exceeding the applicable threshold must be registered through the Firm event process.

Internal gifts may be arranged by the office or practice for appropriate occasions. Gifts not sponsored by the office or practice are personal and not reimbursable. Client codes may not be used for internal gifts.

External gifts must comply with the Anti-Corruption Policy. Client gifts are strongly discouraged. Public-sector client gifts are prohibited. Cash and cash-equivalent gifts are not permitted.

Other expense rules include:

- Personally sourced telecommunications devices or service are not reimbursable.
- IT and telecom equipment claims require the applicable IT approval process.
- Professional publications and research reports require prior knowledge team or responsible partner approval.
- Professional membership fees are generally not reimbursable unless necessary for the Firm role.
- External capabilities, expert networks, outsourced research, and surveys must use approved providers.
- Office equipment and supplies should be obtained through Firm support departments.
- Conference and networking expenses require appropriate reach and relevance approval.
- Visa fees for business travel are reimbursable.
- Passport fees and renewals are personal expenses.
- Engagement-related delivery and postage are reimbursable.
- Bank and currency fees are reimbursable; card late fees are not.
- Additional luggage must be reasonable, necessary, and explained.
- Lost or stolen cash, fines, clothing expenses without approval, and outpatient medication claims are not reimbursable through expenses.

Exceptions are expected to be rare and require the required finance and leadership approvals.

Travel to restricted jurisdictions requires case-by-case approval. Firm members carrying items beyond personal luggage between countries should seek guidance in advance.
