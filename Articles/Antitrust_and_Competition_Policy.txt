Antitrust and Competition Law Policy

This policy applies to all Firm members wherever located and at all times, including informal and social occasions.

Firm members may not enter into anticompetitive agreements, share competitively sensitive information with competitors, or assist clients in anticompetitive activity.

Firm members may not enter no-poach or compensation-fixing agreements or share competitively sensitive employment and compensation information with competitors for talent.

Firm members must escalate potential violations of this policy, including violations by third parties.

The Firm is committed to competing vigorously, honestly, and in compliance with antitrust and competition laws.

A competitor is any company that may provide a service, solution, or offering that a client might choose instead of the Firm's offering. Companies may also be competitors for talent when they recruit or hire employees from the same labor market.

Whether a company is a competitor can change by situation. A company may be a competitor, partner, vendor, or client at different times. Firm members should contact Legal or Antitrust Compliance when unsure.

Competitively sensitive information is confidential information about an entity's current or future market conduct that a competitor could use to its own advantage. Examples include:

- Pricing.
- Costs.
- Production information and plans.
- Commercial terms.
- Customer or client names.
- Business strategies.

Firm members may not enter agreements with competitors on:

- Rates, fees, or fee structures.
- Terms and conditions of client agreements.
- Whether to bid, not bid, or how to structure a bid.
- Specific clients, service areas, or geographies.

Legitimate joint bids or subcontracting with competitors require Legal review.

Firm members may not exchange competitively sensitive information with competitors unless the exchange is part of a legitimate business collaboration or strategic transaction that has been reviewed by Legal.

Firm members may not advise, facilitate, or participate in client anticompetitive conduct, including:

- Abusive or restrictive practices by a dominant market participant.
- Anticompetitive agreements with competitors.
- Coordination of market conduct with competitors.
- Sharing competitively sensitive information with competitors.

Firm members who receive competitively sensitive information during an engagement may not disclose it to a competitor of that party. Potential staffing conflicts involving competitors or competitively sensitive information must be raised before staffing.

Firm members may not enter no-poach agreements with competitors for talent. Limited narrow non-solicitation agreements may be allowed only when reviewed by Legal and reasonably related to a legitimate collaboration or transaction.

Firm members may not agree with competitors for talent on salaries, bonuses, benefits, or other compensation terms.

Firm members may not exchange competitively sensitive employment or compensation information with competitors for talent outside a legitimate reviewed collaboration or strategic transaction.

Competitively sensitive employment and compensation information includes:

- Organizational structures.
- Salaries.
- Bonus levels.
- Benefits.
- Compensation strategies and plans.

Legal must be involved at the earliest opportunity for mergers, acquisitions, joint ventures, and partnership activity. Before closing a transaction with a competitor, interactions must avoid prohibited agreements and improper exchanges of competitively sensitive information.

If a competitor, third party, or client suggests anticompetitive conduct or improper information exchange, Firm members must immediately escalate to Legal or Antitrust Compliance. If immediate rejection is required before consultation, Firm members should reject the conduct and then promptly escalate.

Firm communications must accurately reflect the Firm's commitment to lawful competition. Firm members must not use ambiguous, misleading, or false language that could suggest anticompetitive intent or conduct.

Firm members must comply with this policy, act with integrity, and report violations. If local law is stricter, Firm members must follow local law.

The Firm does not tolerate retaliation for good-faith reporting or participation in a review or investigation.

Failure to comply may result in disciplinary action, including separation from the Firm.
