Travel and Expense Standard - Greater China

This standard supplements the global Travel and Expense Policy and applies to Firm members based in Greater China.

Only actual incurred expenses are reimbursable. Prepaid value cards, estimated expenses, and per diem expenses are not reimbursable.

Expense submission rules include:

- Use central billing when available.
- Use a corporate card when central billing is unavailable and the card is accepted.
- Use personal payment only where permitted.
- Mainland China staff in Mainland China and Taiwan staff in Taiwan must obtain local tax fapiao.
- Other colleagues may submit merchant receipts.
- Full hotel bills and hotel booking confirmation letters must be submitted with hotel claims.

Firm members should make sustainable travel choices. Rail is preferred to air where possible.

When staffed in a city less than a six-hour flight from home, weekly travel home is generally permitted. When staffed farther away, travel frequency should be discussed with the engagement director and is typically every two to four weeks.

Travel approvals depend on role, code type, and whether the travel is client-related or non-client-related.

Rail and air travel rules include:

- Mainland China first-class rail may be booked; partners and associate partners may book business class rail where permitted.
- Taiwan high-speed rail business class is allowed.
- Economy class applies to flights under four hours.
- Business class may be booked for flights over four hours on client codes.
- Additional role-based business-class permissions may apply for domestic or regional travel.
- Flight thresholds are based on total flight time, excluding layovers.
- Flight Wi-Fi is reimbursable up to the applicable limit.

Accommodation rules include:

- Hotel costs are reimbursable when traveling out of town on Firm business.
- Preferred properties booked through the Firm travel service must be used where available.
- Firm hotel rates are for Firm members only.
- If no suitable preferred hotel is available, the travel service should book within the destination's maximum allowed rate.
- In-town hotel stays are generally not reimbursable.
- Exceptions may apply when the client location is outside the city center and the commute would exceed the stated threshold.
- Long-term stays should generally use hotels; serviced apartments may be possible with approval.
- Airbnb and equivalent services are not permitted.
- Hotel invoices should list the Firm home office address, not the traveler's personal address.
- No-show charges are not reimbursable except in exceptional circumstances.

Hotel service reimbursement rules include:

- Hotel internet is reimbursable if not included in the rate.
- Hotel telephone expenses are not reimbursable.
- Laundry is reimbursable for stays of more than four consecutive nights within the stated limit and for business attire only.
- In-hotel entertainment, movies, pay TV, gym fees, spa, and health-club facilities are not reimbursable.
- Breakfast is reimbursable within the stated limit if not included in the hotel rate.
- Room service is reimbursable within individual meal limits.
- Mini-bar expenses are counted within daily meal limits.
- Business center expenses such as meeting rooms, printing, and copying may be reimbursable.

Ground transportation rules include:

- Transportation between home and home office is reimbursable only outside business hours, on weekends, or on public holidays.
- Transportation between home and client is reimbursable.
- Transportation between office and client is reimbursable.
- Preferred ground transportation providers with direct billing must be used where available.
- Premium car services should be used only in exceptional cases.
- Self-drive rental cars are discouraged and require approval for exceptions.
- Personal car business trips are not reimbursable because of local tax rules.

Meal rules include:

- Preferred meal providers with direct billing must be used where available.
- Travel meals outside the home location are reimbursable within local limits.
- Hotel individual meals and room service are reimbursable within higher hotel meal limits.
- Breakfast is reimbursable if not included in the hotel rate.
- Attendee names must be listed when dining with colleagues or clients.
- Meals in the home office on client codes may be reimbursable within limits.
- Non-client home-office meals are generally not reimbursable unless overtime or weekend conditions apply.
- Meals and entertainment with clients must comply with the Anti-Corruption Policy.
- Lower limits apply to meals with government or state-linked company officials.
- Team milestone meals or events should be reasonable and typically no more than once a month.
- Partner attendance or approval is expected for team celebrations.
- Snacks for a client project team room may be expensed and offset meal allowance.
- Meals delivered to or eaten at home are not reimbursable.

Weekend travel rules include:

- If a Firm member stays in the destination city instead of traveling home, corporate hotel and meal expenses are reimbursable over the weekend.
- Other weekend costs, such as ground transportation and attraction entry, are personal.
- Significant-other travel may be covered after the first full week in the study location if another week of work is required, the engagement director is notified, the travel is on a client code, and the cost is lower than travel home.
- Only the significant other's rail or flight cost is covered; other costs are personal and tax treatment may apply.
- Leveraged fly-out travel to a non-home location may be possible on client codes when approved and lower than the cost of returning home. Only economy transportation is covered.

Gift rules include:

- Internal gifts may be arranged by the office or practice for appropriate occasions and should stay below the stated value limit.
- Gifts to colleagues must not use client codes.
- Gift cards are not reimbursable.
- External gifts to clients are discouraged.
- Modest client gifts may be reimbursable in rare circumstances.
- Lower limits and pre-approval apply for government or state-linked company officials.
- Cash and cash equivalents are never permitted as gifts.

Events must be registered when they exceed the applicable threshold and require overnight stay. Meeting planning services should be used for larger events. Practice events and client events require appropriate approvals and should be reasonable, business-oriented, and consistent with risk guidance.

Telecommunications and IT rules include:

- Firm members should use economical and practical means for telephone and data expenses.
- If a Firm phone and subscription are provided, other telecom claims should not be submitted.
- Personal business phone claims may be reimbursable only for eligible colleagues with approval and within limits.
- Loaner phones or MiFi devices should be used for longer international travel with heavy voice or data needs.
- Personal telecom devices are not reimbursable.
- Non-standard IT equipment must be requested through the standard Firm process.

Other expense rules include:

- Business visas should use authorized visa agencies. Tourist visas are not allowed for business travel.
- Passport and personal ID application fees are not reimbursable.
- Work permit-related fees in China are reimbursable.
- Required travel immunizations and testing may be reimbursable.
- Engagement-related shipping is reimbursable.
- Stationery is not reimbursable through expenses.
- Only Firm-approved printing vendors may be used for confidentiality reasons.
- Research reports may be purchased within the stated approval and amount limits.
- External capabilities, expert network services, outsourced research, and surveys must use approved providers.
- Part-time assistants must be contracted through the approved process.
- Translation and interpretation services must use approved vendors.
- Replacement of personal items or money lost or stolen on a business trip is not reimbursable.
- Foreign exchange and overseas bank charges may be reimbursable with documentation.

Travel to restricted jurisdictions requires case-by-case approval. Firm members carrying items beyond personal luggage between countries should seek guidance in advance.
