Gift, Meal, Entertainment, and Travel Expense Thresholds Checker

This reference sets pre-approval thresholds for gifts, meals, entertainment, travel, hotel, and ground transportation expenses provided to government officials and other recipients.

Thresholds vary by country or geographic region and by recipient type. Government officials generally have lower thresholds and stricter requirements than other recipients.

For many countries, the standard government official threshold is:

- US $50 for gifts, meals, entertainment, or ground transportation.
- Pre-approval for all other travel or hotel expenses.
- US $200 per person aggregate expenses per calendar year.

For many countries, the standard threshold for all other recipients is:

- US $350 for gifts or meals.
- US $500 for entertainment.
- US $50 for ground transportation.
- Pre-approval for all other travel or hotel expenses.
- US $1,000 per person aggregate expenses per calendar year.

Some countries require advance pre-approval for all expenses to government officials.

Some countries have lower local-currency thresholds for government officials. Examples include:

- Azerbaijan, where pre-approval is required above the stated local-currency threshold.
- Brazil, where pre-approval is required above the stated local-currency threshold.
- Mainland China, where pre-approval is required above the stated local-currency threshold.
- Singapore, where gifts and meals above the stated local-currency threshold require pre-approval and all entertainment and travel expenses require pre-approval.
- South Africa, where gifts and meals above the stated local-currency threshold require pre-approval and all entertainment requires pre-approval.
- South Korea, where the government official threshold also applies to employees of private media outlets and private schools.
- Spain, where pre-approval is required above the stated local-currency threshold.
- Taiwan, where pre-approval is required above the stated local-currency threshold.

Some countries or regions prohibit gifts, meals, entertainment, or travel to government officials, or require contact with the anti-corruption compliance team before proceeding.

Restricted jurisdictions require special handling. Gifts, meals, entertainment, and travel may be prohibited and the compliance team must be contacted before any activity.

If a Firm member has already provided gifts, meals, entertainment, travel, or hotel expenses to a recipient during the calendar year, additional expenses may require advance approval even if a single new expense is below the ordinary threshold.

Firm members must check the applicable country or region threshold before offering, giving, or reimbursing anything of value.

When the recipient is a government official, public-sector employee, state-owned or state-linked enterprise employee, political party representative, public international organization employee, or similar covered person, the government official threshold should be applied unless Compliance provides different guidance.

If a country-specific rule says to contact Compliance, Firm members should not proceed until Compliance guidance is obtained.
