Human Rights Policy

This policy applies to all Firm members. All Firm members are responsible for their own actions and for the Firm's compliance with this policy.

The Firm aspires to meet the highest professional, legal, and ethical standards, including on human rights. The Firm is committed to a merit-based work environment that supports, inspires, and respects all Firm members.

The policy affirms the Firm's commitment to human rights and to avoiding contribution to, participation in, or enabling of:

- Child labor.
- Forced labor.
- Exploited labor.
- Forced or exploitative conditions.
- Assistance to clients in engaging in these practices.

As a participant in the United Nations Global Compact, the Firm supports:

- The Ten Principles on human rights, labor, environment, and anti-corruption.
- The Universal Declaration of Human Rights.
- The International Labour Organization Declaration on Fundamental Principles and Rights at Work.
- The United Nations Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights.

The Firm strives to follow these principles as they apply to the Firm and its services. The Firm also complies with applicable laws wherever it operates.

The Firm respects Firm members' ability to agree voluntarily to terms and conditions of employment without coercion and to freely end employment with appropriate notice.

The Firm supports the elimination of all forms of forced and compulsory labor, the abolition of child labor, and the elimination of human trafficking.

The Firm is committed to complying with laws regulating minimum working age for each role, including laws related to employment, apprenticeships, internships, youths, and students.

The Firm strictly prohibits:

- Forced labor.
- Exploitative working conditions.
- Unlawful eviction or seizure of property.
- Use of unconstrained security forces in a way that damages public health.

Forced labor means work or service extracted from a person under threat of penalty and not offered voluntarily.

The Firm's commitment to human rights also applies to supplier relationships through the Supplier Code of Conduct.

Questions or concerns about this policy should be directed to the office manager, Firm Legal, or HR representative.

Every Firm member may raise ethical concerns in good faith without fear of retaliation, punishment, or harassment.

Firm members must comply with this policy, act with integrity, and report violations. If local law is stricter, Firm members must follow local law.

Failure to comply may result in disciplinary action, including separation from the Firm. For third parties engaged by the Firm, violations may result in consequences up to and including termination of the arrangement.
