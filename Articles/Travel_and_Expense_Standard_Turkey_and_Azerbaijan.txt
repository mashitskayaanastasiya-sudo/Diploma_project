Travel and Expense Rules and Thresholds Standard - Turkey and Azerbaijan

This standard supplements and is enforceable under the Travel and Expense Policy. It applies to Firm members assigned to Turkey and Azerbaijan.

Expense claims must be submitted within 60 days of incurring the expense. December expenses may need to be submitted earlier to support financial year-end close.

All travel expense claims must include charge codes and FMNOs.

Only actual incurred expenses are reimbursable. Pre-paid value cards, estimated expenses, and per diem expenses are not reimbursable unless specifically allowed.

Full hotel bills must be submitted with hotel expense claims and addressed to the relevant COMPANY office address.

Full detailed receipts are required with all expense submissions unless the standard states otherwise. Cash payments require receipts showing who was paid and the city and country of payment. Original receipts and invoices must be submitted to local Finance where required.

Where a corporate card is available, eligible Firm members must obtain one before travel and use it for all business expenses where accepted. Firm members are responsible for managing corporate card accounts and timely payment. Corporate cards must not be used for personal expenses.

Firm members who are not eligible for a corporate card may use a personal payment method for reimbursable business expenses.

When travel is required, Firm members should make sustainable choices to reduce environmental impact.

Air travel rules:

- Domestic client-related air travel is Economy class.
- International client-related air travel is Business class if the flight is at least four hours; otherwise Economy class.
- APs and Partners may travel in Business class because they travel regularly throughout the year.
- Istanbul-Baku flights are Economy class except for one-night accommodation trips or health issues with ED approval.
- Non-client travel is Economy class for all roles and requires Office Manager approval, excluding approved learning or training.
- Non-client travel should be booked at least two weeks in advance using restricted or non-refundable tickets.
- Event, internal meeting, or learning-program travel guidelines supersede this standard where applicable.

Business class thresholds are based on total flight time and exclude layover time. If a non-stop flight is available, the cabin allowed on the non-stop flight determines eligibility.

Travel must be booked through Firm Travel by contacting the travel service for the assigned COMPANY office. Requests must include FMNO or EWNO, charge code, and required booking details.

Hotel invoices should be submitted and accepted only after checkout. Direct billing is preferred. Where direct billing is unavailable, payment by the travel management company may be allowed with Local Admin Team approval.

For Baku external workers, onboarded external workers must use their official Firm email address when contacting travel management, and Local Admin must be copied on approval requests.

In-town accommodation is not reimbursable except in force majeure situations such as emergency displacement from a primary residence or other urgent and justified cases with Office Manager approval. Requests should be justified in writing, supported where applicable, and approved before booking when possible.

Location-specific accommodation may be reimbursable only if the client or engagement location is at least 50 km from the home office and written approval from the project ED is obtained.

Preferred hotels must be used where available and booked through Firm Travel. If no preferred hotel is available or suitable, travel service will offer a hotel within the city maximum rate.

Extended stay or corporate apartments may be offered by Firm Travel. Arrangements over one month require applicable approval and must cost less than hotel accommodation for the business nights.

No-show charges are not reimbursable except in exceptional circumstances such as sudden illness, client urgency, severe weather, or transportation disruption. Exceptional cases require written justification and ED or Office Manager approval.

Other hotel services:

- Laundry may be reimbursable for stays of at least five nights or unexpected schedule changes.
- Room service is reimbursable under meal rules.
- Minibar expenses are reimbursable except alcohol.
- Ironing or pressing shirts is reimbursable.
- Gyms outside the hotel and spa treatments are not reimbursable.

Ground transportation rules:

- Preferred modes include taxi, ride-hailing, personal car mileage, and public transport where a Firm car is not provided.
- Direct-billing taxi and ride-hailing requests must include FMNO and charge code.
- Home-to-office transportation is reimbursable in Turkey only for FSP and ESP on Fridays, public holidays, and after 8 p.m. on weekdays. It is reimbursable in Azerbaijan.
- Home-to-client and office-to-client transportation is reimbursable.
- Third-party transportation services are reimbursable only for exceptional client engagements with ED approval.
- Airport ground transportation is reimbursable.
- Rental cars are not recommended and require prior approval from office Finance. Luxury vehicles are not reimbursable.
- Personal car mileage is reimbursable for business travel at the local rate, except for those with a Firm-provided car and car allowance.

Parking at client sites and assigned COMPANY locations is reimbursable. Bridge, highway, fuel, and toll charges are reimbursable for Firm cars, excluding personal cars.

Meal limits:

- Turkey: daily individual meal limit applies in local currency.
- Azerbaijan: daily individual meal limit applies in local currency.
- Other countries: use the meal limits of the destination office, or the fallback daily limit where there is no COMPANY office.
- Team meals and team events have office-specific per-colleague limits and engagement-cycle limits.
- Group meals with non-Firm members require Anti-Corruption review and any necessary approvals before the expense is incurred.
- SOE and public sector client meals require GMET pre-approval regardless of amount.
- The bill-payer must submit the expense correctly in Rydoo, attach GMET approval where applicable, and include a full list of team and client attendees.

Weekend travel home is allowed weekly. If the Firm member stays in the work city for the weekend, hotel and meal expenses may be reimbursed up to the cost of returning home.

Spouse or significant other travel to the work city may be reimbursed within the cap based on the cost of returning home. The flight must be restricted Economy, the lowest available fare, and booked by Firm travel service. Other spouse expenses and all child expenses are personal.

Travel to an alternative non-home city is not allowed because of taxation risk.

Internal gifts are arranged by the office for specific occasions. Gifts to colleagues not sponsored by the office are not reimbursable, and gift cards are not reimbursable.

External gifts must comply with the Anti-Corruption Policy. External gifts require the Gift Approval Form, ED approval, and where applicable Ethics & Compliance approval through the GMET pre-approval tool.

Only authorized local leadership may initiate and approve client gift arrangements.

Events over the local equivalent of US$20,000 that require an overnight stay must be registered through Optimize Events.

Other expenses:

- Food and taxi tips up to 10 percent may be reimbursed if shown on the bill.
- Loaner mobile devices can be obtained through GHD; personally sourced telecom services are not reimbursable.
- Home phone and internet are not reimbursable.
- IT supplies require GHD approval.
- Professional publications and market research require practice knowledge team approval.
- Professional membership fees and ongoing education fees are generally not reimbursable unless necessary for the role.
- Office supplies should be obtained from office services where possible.
- PPE purchases require written ED approval.
- Conferences and networking expenses are reimbursable only with cell Reach and Relevance leadership approval.
- Business visa fees are reimbursable.
- Passport renewal due to expiration is personal; renewal due to insufficient pages from Firm travel is reimbursable.
- Engagement-related delivery and postage are reimbursable.
- Credit card late fees are not reimbursable.

Exceptions are expected to be rare and require written Office Manager approval.

Travel safety requirements follow the Travel Safety Standard. If a Firm member becomes sick while traveling on Firm business, they should contact the Firm's travel medical assistance company.

Travel to Restricted Jurisdictions may be approved only case by case. Carrying items beyond personal luggage across countries may require advance authorization or customs payments.
