Quick Guide to Anti-Corruption Pre-Approvals

Bribes can take many forms, including gifts, meals, entertainment, and travel. If gifts, meals, entertainment, or travel could be used to improperly influence a decision maker in connection with the Firm, the Firm may be legally responsible.

Interactions with government officials present elevated risk.

Government officials are defined broadly and include:

- Candidates for political office.
- Officials, employees, or members of a government.
- Political parties.
- Public international organizations.
- State-owned or state-linked enterprises.

Before offering gifts, meals, entertainment, or travel to non-Firm members, Firm members should identify the purpose of the activity. The purpose helps determine what is appropriate, such as a working dinner rather than a celebration.

Any gifts, meals, entertainment, or travel must:

- Be reasonable in value.
- Be given openly.
- Align with the Code of Conduct.
- Not be intended to influence decision makers improperly.
- Comply with local travel and expense limits and other applicable requirements.

Firm members should check whether planned gifts, meals, entertainment, or travel with non-Firm members may exceed pre-approval thresholds. Thresholds for government officials are often lower and may change.

If pre-approval is required, Firm members should estimate the per-person value. If the value is difficult to quantify, they should contact Anti-Corruption Compliance.

Pre-approval must be requested through the gifts, meals, entertainment, and travel pre-approval process. Providing gifts, meals, entertainment, or travel to a non-Firm member's family, spouse, significant other, or guest is generally discouraged.

Anti-Corruption Compliance may ask follow-up questions and will send a final decision indicating whether the request is pre-approved or rejected.

Approved activity must stay within the pre-approved scope and amount. Final expenses should not be subsidized with personal payments. Expenses are subject to audit and Compliance monitoring.

Any gifts, meals, entertainment, or travel activity conducted without required pre-approval should be raised to Anti-Corruption Compliance for resolution.

Expense reimbursement requests should include the pre-approval ID and the pre-approval email with the receipt. If the expense involved government officials, that should be identified in the expense system.

Firm members must immediately report suspected bribery, facilitation payment requests, received bribes, or suspected policy violations, including violations by third parties.

Events involving non-Firm members often include elements governed by the Anti-Corruption Policy and the gifts, meals, entertainment, and travel procedure.

Firm members should complete required pre-approval steps or contact Anti-Corruption Compliance before sending invitations when an event involves:

- Government officials, including public-sector clients, state-owned or state-linked entities, or Firm alumni in government positions.
- Meals, drinks, entertainment, gifts, travel, hotels, or ground transportation for government officials that may exceed thresholds.
- Travel, airfare, hotel, or ground transportation for any non-Firm member.
- Per-person gifts, meals, entertainment, or travel costs that exceed policy thresholds.
- Waived event registration fees above applicable thresholds.
- External events where the Firm is a knowledge partner and sponsors similar program costs.
- Non-Firm member spouses, significant others, or family members.
- Charitable donations or charitable sponsorships, including donations in lieu of paying an event speaker.
- Events involving competitors.

If the primary purpose of a meeting is to bring competitors in an industry together, such as an industry roundtable or forum, the event requires an antitrust reminder and antitrust compliance review of planned agendas.
