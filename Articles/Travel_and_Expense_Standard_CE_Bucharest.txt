Travel and Expense Standard - CE Bucharest

This standard supplements the Travel and Expense Policy and applies to Firm members assigned to Bucharest.

Only actual incurred expenses are reimbursable. Prepaid value cards, estimated expenses, and per diem expenses are not reimbursable except where expressly allowed.

Expense submission rules include:

- Receipts are required for all expenses.
- Cash receipts must show who was paid and the city and country of payment.
- Payment terminal slips and bank statements are not accepted as receipts.
- Original receipts should be provided to Accounting where required.
- Expenses are reimbursed in RON.

Firm members should make sustainable travel choices when travel is required.

Air and rail travel rules include:

- Client-related business class is permitted only when the flight meets the stated duration and weekly flight time thresholds.
- Non-client travel defaults to economy class.
- Non-client travel should be booked at least two weeks in advance using restricted or non-refundable tickets.
- Business class for non-client travel may be permitted for longer flights with budget owner approval.
- Business-class thresholds are based on total flight time, excluding layovers.
- Rail travel defaults to economy class.
- Rail business class may be booked if it costs less than economy air travel for a comparable itinerary.

Accommodation rules include:

- Accommodation for Bucharest Firm members is generally paid by the travel agent and reinvoiced to the office.
- Preferred hotels must be used where available and booked through Firm Travel or an approved booking tool.
- Accommodation costs in the home city are not reimbursable.
- If no suitable preferred hotel is available, the travel service should book within the city's maximum allowed rate.
- Extended stays or corporate apartments may be offered by the travel service when cost effective and approved where required.
- Airbnb is not permitted.
- No-show charges are not reimbursable except in exceptional circumstances.

Hotel service reimbursement rules include:

- Laundry is reimbursable for stays of two nights or more.
- Hotel telephone is reimbursable only when other options are not available.
- Pay TV, mini-bar, room service, gyms outside the hotel, and spa treatments are not reimbursable.

Ground transportation rules include:

- Transportation between home and home office is reimbursable only outside business hours, before 7:00 a.m., after 9:00 p.m., or on public holidays.
- Transportation between home and client is reimbursable.
- Transportation between office and client is reimbursable.
- Preferred modes include public transport, taxi, and ride-hail vendors.
- Airport and intercity transfer services may be centrally billed where available.
- Personal car expenses, parking, tolls, and rental cars are not reimbursable.

Meal rules include:

- Receipts must show all items ordered.
- Only receipts from restaurants, fast food providers, or cafeterias are accepted.
- Supermarket and grocery receipts are not accepted.
- Client room supplies are not reimbursable.
- Individual meals outside the home city are reimbursable up to the stated daily limit.
- Meals at a client location within Romania require a valid client charge code.
- Weekend and late meals may be reimbursable when working more than three hours under the stated conditions.
- Team meals and team events are reimbursable within frequency, amount, and approval expectations.
- Alcohol is limited to wine and beer with meals unless exceptionally approved.
- Liquor, shots, tobacco, gifts, and tangible goods are not reimbursable.
- Group meals with non-Firm members must comply with the Anti-Corruption Policy and may require approvals.
- Tips are reimbursable if stated on the receipt.

Weekend travel rules include:

- Travel home frequency depends on region and direct flight time.
- Staying in the work city instead of traveling home may be allowed within the same frequency and may reimburse hotel and meal expenses up to the cost of returning home.
- Spouse or significant-other travel and leveraged fly-out travel are treated as personal-purpose travel and may have limited reimbursement only within the stated rules.
- Required supporting documents must be submitted to Accounting by the relevant deadline.

Events requiring overnight stay and exceeding the applicable threshold must be registered through the Firm event process.

Internal gifts may be arranged by the office or practice for specific occasions. Gifts to colleagues not sponsored by the office or practice are not reimbursable. Gift cards are not reimbursable.

External gifts to clients must comply with the Anti-Corruption Policy. Additional rules apply to government officials and public-sector clients or potential clients.

Other expense rules include:

- Personally sourced telecommunications devices or service are not reimbursable.
- Home phone and internet are not reimbursable.
- IT supplies require approval through the relevant IT process.
- Professional publications and market research require prior knowledge team or research approval.
- Professional membership fees are generally not reimbursable unless necessary for the Firm role.
- Office supplies should be obtained from office services when possible.
- Conference and networking expenses should be billed to and paid by the office directly.
- Visa fees for business travel are reimbursable.
- Ordinary passport renewal is personal; renewal due to insufficient pages from extensive Firm travel may be reimbursable.
- Delivery and postage are not reimbursable through expenses and should be arranged through the local office.
- Business-related bank currency fees may be reimbursed with documentation.
- Required client-travel testing may be reimbursable.
- Required travel immunizations may be reimbursable if not covered by insurance.
- Lost items and delivery of items left in hotels are personal expenses.
- Gym or massage subscription reimbursement may apply within local limits and documentation requirements.
- In-flight Wi-Fi is reimbursable when client-related or on long-haul flights.

Exceptions are expected to be rare and require written approval from the appropriate leader.

Travel to restricted jurisdictions requires case-by-case approval. Firm members carrying items beyond personal luggage between countries should seek guidance in advance.
