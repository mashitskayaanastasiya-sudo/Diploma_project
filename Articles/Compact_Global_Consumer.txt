Compact Global Consumer

This compact does not supersede other Firm policies or guidelines. Partners and CSTs remain responsible for complying with the Client Service Policy, M&A and Serving Competitors guidance, and any other relevant sector compacts for the specific client or engagement context.

Work that is not approved includes:

- Work on tobacco-related business issues, including vaping and nicotine.
- Cannabis work, including CBD work, where it is unlawful or unregulated.
- Lobbying for companies or industry and trade associations.
- Work that could have a nexus to the manufacture or sale of firearms, ammunition, or related accessories.
- Any engagement, regardless of topic, with an institution where a material portion of the business comes from manufacturing or selling firearms or ammunition.

High-risk work requires CSRC approval with input from PRACL and other relevant cell leaders, as well as ongoing deliverable review. Examples include:

- Work related to cannabis where it is lawful and regulated.
- Supply chain work for clients facing forced labor litigation or a state investigation, such as a UFLPA inquiry.
- Firm-hosted solutions or software deployments that may touch service-line boundaries or higher-risk products, including tobacco, firearms, ammunition, cannabis, or THC.
- Work that could have a nexus to manufacturing or selling tobacco products.

Medium-risk plus work requires CSRC approval with input from PRACL and other relevant cell leaders. Examples include:

- Work related to CBD where it is lawful and regulated.
- Work with clients, especially LEAP engagements, related to emerging cosmetic, medical, and food technologies, such as biologics, novel cosmetic ingredients, doctor-backed beauty, OTC vitamins or supplements, wearable healthcare technology, or alternative proteins.
- Veterinary health work.
- Engagements where the client or engagement is likely to face legal or regulatory scrutiny, especially work on a topic where the client is actively under investigation.
- Work related to prescription medications for weight loss, including GLP-1 receptor agonists such as Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, or Zepbound.
- Pricing work in categories experiencing shortages or supply chain disruptions.

Medium-risk work requires PRACL approval with input from PRACL and other relevant cell leaders. Examples include:

- Labor-related operational or strategic topics where unionization or work councils may be relevant.
- Supply chain work with clients facing forced labor allegations without formal litigation or state investigation, including allegations from mass media or human rights organizations.
- Work that could relate to gambling, sports betting, or lotteries.
- Work with companies engaged in multi-level marketing.

Additional consumer risk topics may require review under other Firm policies and standards, including:

- Alcohol.
- GLP-1 receptor agonists.
- Health, safety, and customer product quality.
- Industry and trade associations.
- Mergers, acquisitions, and alliances.
- Pricing.
- Workforce reduction.
- Unions and work councils.
- CoRE.
- Technology delivery.
- Processing personal data in client engagements.
- External interviews.
- Mystery shopping and store exit interviews.
- Surveys.
- Confidentiality policies.
- Joint engagements and client teams.
- Client or third-party introductions.
- Third-party interactions during engagements.
- Purchaser-supplier engagements.
- Client-facing collaborations.
- Research collaborations.
- Use of name.
- Equity and value-based fees.
- Interim roles.

Teams should review the relevant sector compact and Firm policy before proceeding with consumer work that touches higher-risk topics.
